Where to Stay in Da Nang

Da Nang is compact — but each neighbourhood delivers a different holiday entirely. This guide maps five distinct areas so you can choose the one that fits your trip, not just the one with the most reviews.

Da Nang stretches roughly 30 kilometres along Vietnam's central coast, but the city itself is manageable — you can cross it by taxi in 20 minutes on a clear morning. What changes dramatically between neighbourhoods isn't distance; it's feel. A hotel on My Khe Beach puts you in a confident beach-resort environment with everything accessible on foot. The same budget on Son Tra Peninsula lands you in protected jungle above a private bay, an entirely different context from the same city. Neither experience is better in absolute terms — but choosing the wrong one for your trip is the most common mistake visitors make.

My Khe Beach — Da Nang's Main Strip

The default base for most international visitors, and for good reason: direct beach access, a walkable restaurant and café scene, and hotels at every price point within a 10-minute walk of each other.

My Khe is where Da Nang's beach reputation was built, and the infrastructure around it has kept pace with its popularity without entirely losing character. The stretch between the Marriott at the north and the Pullman at the south is the densest — dozens of mid-range and upscale hotels sit within three blocks of the sand, with the independently-run An Thuong village area behind them offering coffee shops, Vietnamese street food, and the kind of neighbourhood feel that resort strips usually work hard to simulate.

The beach itself is well-maintained — the city funds daily cleaning and the flagging system for safe swimming zones is taken seriously. Between March and August the sea is calm enough for children; from September the northeast monsoon brings choppier conditions and occasional beach closures.

Best for: First-time visitors, couples on 4–7 night beach holidays, anyone who wants good food and nightlife within walking distance of their hotel. Also the most practical base for day trips: Hoi An is 40 minutes south, Ba Na Hills 40 minutes southwest.

🌇 Best for nightlife & city life

Han River Area — Da Nang's Urban Core

The city's commercial and cultural spine. Staying on the Han River puts you within walking distance of Dragon Bridge, the MICHELIN dining scene, the night market, and Da Nang's best independent coffee shops.

The Han River has been Da Nang's focal point since the city's colonial era, and the riverside promenade remains its most atmospheric evening destination — particularly on weekends when Dragon Bridge performs at 9pm and the road closes to traffic. The hotels here aren't on the beach, but the city energy more than compensates, and My Khe is a short taxi or Grab ride away.

The dining options within walking distance are the strongest in the city. Several of Da Nang's MICHELIN-recognised restaurants are clustered in the Hai Chau district; the night market on Bach Dang Street runs Tuesday through Sunday. For those who find resort beaches enjoyable but resort atmospheres stifling, basing yourself here and treating My Khe as a day-trip destination is a genuinely satisfying approach.

Best for: Business travellers, short stays, couples who prioritise food and culture over sand time, and anyone using Da Nang primarily as a base for the wider Central Vietnam region.

🌿 Best for luxury & seclusion

Son Tra Peninsula — Da Nang's Resort Jewel

A protected nature reserve 20 minutes north of the city where Da Nang's most acclaimed resorts sit above private bays in forested hillsides. An entirely different experience from the beach strip below.

Son Tra is technically part of Da Nang, but it operates as its own world. The peninsula is a gazetted nature reserve — development is strictly limited, the monkey population is genuinely significant, and the roads wind through dense tropical forest before opening onto clifftop views of the South China Sea. The properties here have had to become self-sufficient destinations because once you're up the mountain, leaving requires intent.

The InterContinental Sun Peninsula Resort is the anchor property and one of the most discussed hotels in Southeast Asia. La Maison 1888, the on-site restaurant, holds Da Nang's only MICHELIN Star and is worth the trip up for a dinner reservation even if you're staying elsewhere in the city. For anyone staying on Son Tra, the resort's beach shuttle, boat transfers, and activity programme mean you rarely need to think about the city below.

Best for: Honeymoons, milestone celebrations, dedicated resort holidays, and anyone who finds the beach strip atmosphere too busy. Budget minimum is approximately $250 per night; most Son Tra properties run $300–$500 at standard occupancy.

City Centre — Hai Chau & Thanh Khe

The city's workaday interior, where the street food is better, the prices are lower, and the experience of actually being in Vietnam rather than on a tourist strip is the highest in Da Nang.

Staying in the Hai Chau or Thanh Khe districts — the real city centre, away from both the river promenade and the beach — is a choice that pays off for a specific type of traveller. Accommodation is cheaper (guesthouses from $15, decent hotels from $30), the street food is more interesting, and the day-to-day experience involves more actual Vietnamese life. The Cham Museum, Da Nang Market, and the city's best bánh mì shops are all within walking distance.

The beach requires a taxi or motorbike ride — 15 to 20 minutes depending on traffic. For budget travellers happy to treat the beach as a day-trip destination rather than the backdrop of their stay, this is the most cost-efficient base in Da Nang. For anyone spending significant time in Hoi An (30 minutes south) or Hue (90 minutes north), the central location also reduces transit time considerably.

Best for: Budget-conscious travellers, solo visitors who want local immersion, longer stays where variety matters, and anyone combining Da Nang with significant time elsewhere in Central Vietnam.

🌊 Best for resort-style stays

Non Nuoc Beach — Quieter South Strip

The quieter, more spacious southern extension of Da Nang's coastline. Larger resort properties, fewer vendors, calmer water, Marble Mountains on the doorstep, and Hoi An 40 minutes south.

Non Nuoc starts where My Khe effectively ends — the Marble Mountains are the natural dividing line — and the change in atmosphere is immediate. The beach is wider and less densely developed. Hotels here tend to be larger resort properties with more grounds, multiple pools, and beach programmes that don't require leaving the property for entertainment. It's measurably quieter than My Khe even in peak season.

The Hyatt Regency is the anchor property: six hectares of resort, a beach maintained to a standard My Khe's shared strand can't match, and room quality that holds up against regional competition. For families in particular, the additional space and dedicated kids' facilities make Non Nuoc a more considered choice than the tighter My Khe properties.

The trade-off is distance from the city — 15 to 20 minutes from My Khe's restaurant and café scene, 25 from the Han River. Guests planning to spend most of their time at the resort barely notice; those who want to explore Da Nang extensively may find it inconvenient after a few days. For Marble Mountains and Hoi An in the other direction, however, the location is genuinely convenient.

Best for: Families with young children, resort-focused holidaymakers, guests combining Da Nang with substantial time in Hoi An, and anyone who finds My Khe too busy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best area to stay in Da Nang for first-time visitors?
My Khe Beach is the right call for a first visit. You get direct beach access, a walkable restaurant strip along An Thuong village, and hotels from $35 guesthouses to $200 beachfront resorts. It's also the most practical base for day trips — Hoi An is 40 minutes south, Ba Na Hills 40 minutes southwest, and Marble Mountains 20 minutes away. The beach itself is well-maintained and reliably calm between March and August.
Is Son Tra Peninsula worth staying at?
Yes — if seclusion and resort quality are your priorities. Son Tra is a protected nature reserve 20 minutes from the city centre. The InterContinental Sun Peninsula is the headline property and consistently ranks among the best hotels in Southeast Asia. The trade-off is isolation: you need a taxi or motorbike for everything outside the resort grounds. Best suited to honeymoons, anniversary trips, and anyone who wants a genuine switch-off holiday rather than an active city base.
Is the Han River area good for tourists?
Yes, particularly for city-focused travellers. The Han River strip is walkable, packed with cafés and restaurants — including several MICHELIN-recognised spots — and within walking distance of Dragon Bridge and the night market. The beach is 15 minutes by taxi. It works well for short stays, business trips, and anyone combining Da Nang with significant time in Hoi An who wants urban convenience over a beach base.
Which Da Nang area has the cheapest hotels?
The city centre — specifically the Hai Chau and Thanh Khe districts — has Da Nang's most affordable accommodation, with guesthouses from $15–$25 a night. My Khe Beach has budget options from around $35, typically a block or two back from the sand. The Han River area has mid-range options from $50 that offer river views without the full pricing of the landmark properties.
