Premier Village Danang Resort Review

From $300/night ★ 9.0 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

Premier Village Danang Resort occupies the southern tip of Son Tra Peninsula in an extraordinary natural position — flanked by sea on three sides with views across both My Khe Beach to the south and the Nam O Bay to the north. The resort is an all-villa property: every unit is a private villa with its own pool, outdoor living space, and direct beach or ocean views. This makes it one of the most distinctive accommodation concepts in Da Nang.

Managed by Sun Group, one of Vietnam's largest resort developers, Premier Village has 109 villas ranging from three-bedroom beachfront options to smaller two-bedroom hillside configurations. It is one of the top-rated hotels in Da Nang on every major booking platform and particularly beloved by families who want the privacy of a villa holiday combined with the services of a managed resort.

Location

Son Tra Peninsula is a forested headland north of Da Nang city, within the boundaries of a national nature reserve. Premier Village's position at its southern tip means you are simultaneously near the city (10 km to Dragon Bridge) and surrounded by nature — the Son Tra forest rises behind the resort, and the sea is present in almost every direction. The airport is 13 km away.

This is a location for guests who want to feel removed from Da Nang's urban energy while retaining reasonable access to it. The drive from the resort into the city takes 20–25 minutes. Hoi An is 40 minutes by car.

  • Airport: 13 km / 20 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 22 min taxi
  • My Khe Beach strip: 15 min taxi
  • Son Tra nature reserve: on doorstep
  • Hoi An: 40 min by car

Rooms and Design

All 109 villas have private infinity pools — this is the defining feature of the resort. Sizes range from around 200 to 600 square metres, with the largest beachfront villas accommodating families of six or eight comfortably. Design is sophisticated tropical contemporary, with high ceilings, local stone and timber finishes, and indoor-outdoor living areas that maximise the views.

The two- and three-bedroom beachfront villas are the peak of the portfolio and rival the finest villa stays in Southeast Asia. Even the smaller hillside villas are generous by any reasonable benchmark. All villas include a fully equipped kitchen and dining area.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

Every villa has a private infinity pool, so pool crowding is not a concern. A communal beach club area is available for those who want a social pool environment. The private beach in front of the resort is clean and has full service. A full spa, fitness centre, and kids' club are part of the resort facilities.

Dining Options

The main restaurant, Views Beach Club, serves Vietnamese and international cuisine with a beachfront setting that ranks among the best dining views in Da Nang. A pool bar operates on the beach club level. In-villa dining and a private chef service are available — popular for families celebrating special occasions.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): from $300/night for 2-bed hillside villa
  • Shoulder season: $400–$600/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $550–$950+/night
  • 3-bed beachfront villas: from $700/night in low season

Who This Hotel Is Best For

Premier Village is ideal for families wanting genuine privacy, couples on honeymoon or anniversary trips who want a villa experience, and groups of friends or extended family travelling together who want shared private space. It is less suited to solo travellers or couples who don't need the scale of a full villa.

Pros
  • Every villa has a private infinity pool — genuinely unique in Da Nang
  • Three-sided ocean views from Son Tra Peninsula
  • One of Da Nang's highest-rated hotels on every platform
  • Strong family credentials — large villa sizes, kids' club
  • Private beach with full service
  • Views Beach Club restaurant setting exceptional
Cons
  • High nightly rates — among Da Nang's most expensive
  • Remote feeling — 20+ min to city centre
  • Full resort facilities more limited than large resort complexes
  • Must book well in advance for peak season villas

Final Verdict

Premier Village Danang Resort is one of the finest family villa resorts in all of Vietnam. The combination of private infinity pools, exceptional Son Tra Peninsula views, and genuine beachfront access at a resort with professional management and service is hard to match anywhere in the country. For families or couples who want the ultimate Da Nang villa experience, this is the destination.

Is Premier Village Danang Resort Worth It?

The Premier Village Danang Resort is one of the most unusual properties on My Khe Beach — its pool villa layout gives each unit a private pool, a rarity at this price point in Da Nang. For families, it is arguably the best resort option in the city: children can swim safely in their own villa pool, the beach is directly accessible, and the overall layout gives families genuine privacy. The trade-off is in the resort amenities overall — it lacks the depth of dining and activities of the Hyatt Regency or InterContinental. But for families who will spend most of their time on the beach and around their villa, that gap is largely irrelevant. At rates that are often below the major international chains, it offers outstanding family value.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From Premier Village Danang Resort, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • My Khe Beach — On-site. Direct beachfront access — the villas sit just steps from the sand.
  • Dragon Bridge — 15 min taxi. The city's signature landmark — weekend fire show is a memorable evening out.
  • Han River — 15 min taxi. City-centre riverside with restaurants, cafes, and night markets.
  • Marble Mountains — 20 min taxi south. Five limestone hills with temples and caves — a worthwhile half-day trip.
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — 30 min by car. The essential day trip — ancient town, tailors, and riverside restaurants.
  • My Khe Restaurant Strip — 5 min walk. Fresh seafood grills and local restaurants directly along the beachfront road.
