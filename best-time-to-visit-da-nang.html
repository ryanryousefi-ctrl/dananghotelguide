Da Nang weather overview

Da Nang sits in Vietnam's narrow Central Coast strip â€” sandwiched between the Annamite Mountains to the west and the South China Sea to the east â€” and this geography shapes its climate in a specific way. The mountains block cold air from inland, meaning winters are mild rather than cold, but they also funnel the northeast monsoon directly into the coast from September through November, making that window genuinely difficult for travel.

The result is a climate that most visitors would describe as straightforwardly good for most of the year, with one serious interruption. The dry season running from roughly February through August is long, warm, and beach-functional for six continuous months. The monsoon is the trade-off: October in Da Nang is legitimately challenging â€” monthly rainfall regularly exceeds 600mm, the sea is too rough for swimming, and some years bring typhoon-adjacent storm systems through. Most informed travellers avoid this window specifically.

Da Nang does not have an extreme rainy-season-and-dry-season divide the way Thailand or Bali does â€” the shoulder periods (December through February, and briefly in September) are more nuanced than either/or. Understanding the specifics matters for booking decisions, particularly for beachfront hotel choices. See the Da Nang beach hotels guide for properties that offer specific seasonal flexibility.

Best months to visit Da Nang

If you have flexibility on timing, March through May is the window that experienced Da Nang visitors return to. The dry season is established but the summer crowds haven't arrived, and prices reflect that.

March, April, and May are the sweet spot for most travellers. The northeast monsoon has cleared, temperatures climb steadily from a pleasant 28Â°C in March to a warm 32Â°C by May, and the sea conditions are consistently good for swimming along both My Khe and Non Nuoc beaches. Crucially, these months precede the Vietnamese summer holiday surge â€” July and August bring enormous domestic tourism volumes that fill hotels and crowd the beach by 8am. In March, you can still find sunbeds after breakfast.

Hotel rates in March and April sit at shoulder-season pricing â€” typically 20â€“30% below peak â€” which means you can access the better beachfront properties at prices that represent genuine value. The best hotels in Da Nang across all categories are bookable with advance planning in this window; in July they require 6â€“8 weeks of lead time and higher rates.

February is an honourable mention â€” the tail end of winter brings a few overcast days and occasional light rain, but conditions improve noticeably through the month. The major complication is Tet (Vietnamese New Year, date shifts annually between late January and mid-February), which brings accommodation price spikes, restaurant closures, and significant domestic tourist crowds for the week of the holiday itself. Travel in the week after Tet and you get improving weather, clearing crowds, and a city still in celebration mode without the logistical chaos.

Rainy season: Septemberâ€“November

Da Nang's northeast monsoon is not to be underestimated. October is one of the wettest months of any city in Southeast Asia â€” not just "some afternoon showers" wet, but 600mm-in-a-single-month wet.

Septemberâ€“November: Serious rainfall, rough seas

The monsoon arrives progressively from September, intensifies through October, and begins to ease in November. What this means in practice: September brings heavy daily rain with some beach-functional breaks between storms. October is the month to avoid â€” rainfall averages over 600mm (compare: London's entire annual rainfall is about 600mm), the sea is consistently rough with red flags on the beach, and some storms reach typhoon strength. November sees gradual improvement but remains unreliable.

Hotel rates during this window drop 35â€“50% from peak. The city's food scene, museums, and cultural sites all operate normally â€” if your trip is focused on gastronomy and urban exploration rather than beach time, it can represent strong value. Typhoon risk: Da Nang sits in Vietnam's typhoon corridor. Most years see typhoon-strength systems passing through or near the coast between August and December, with October being the highest-risk month. This is not theoretical â€” significant storm events occur most years and occasionally cause serious flooding in low-lying areas. Check storm tracking forecasts if travelling during this window.

Best time for Da Nang beaches

For beach-focused travel, the window is unambiguous: March through August, with the clearest conditions in March, April, and May. The sea along My Khe and Non Nuoc is calm, clear, and swimmable throughout this period. Water temperatures climb from about 24Â°C in March to a bathwater-warm 29â€“30Â°C in July and August.

The early morning before 8am is underrated regardless of month in the dry season â€” My Khe Beach at 6am in April is quiet, the light is extraordinary, and the sea is placid.
