Da Nang's Beach Zones Explained

Da Nang's 30km coastline divides into two distinct zones — My Khe (north) and Non Nuoc (south) — separated by the Marble Mountains

Da Nang has 30 kilometres of coastline, but not all of it is equal — and the two primary beach zones serve very different travel styles. My Khe Beach (northern stretch) is the main hotel strip: the densest concentration of accommodation, restaurants, and beach infrastructure in the city. Non Nuoc Beach (southern stretch) is quieter, wider, and backed by larger resort properties — a different atmosphere entirely.

The Marble Mountains mark the natural dividing line between the two zones and are themselves worth a half-day visit. Both beach areas are accessible from Da Nang Airport in 15–25 minutes. Neither is far from the other — if you find your first-choice beach lacking, the alternative is a 20-minute taxi ride.

This guide covers the best beachfront hotels in both zones. For the full hotel range across all neighbourhoods, see the Da Nang hotel guide.

Zone 1

My Khe Beach Hotels

The main beach strip and Da Nang's most popular accommodation zone. Hotels here offer direct sand access, walkable restaurant options in the An Thuong village strip, and easy Grab access to the city. The trade-off is higher rates and peak-season crowds.

Zone 2

Non Nuoc Beach Hotels

Quieter, wider, and backed by larger resort compounds. Non Nuoc is the right choice for families with young children (calmer surf), resort-focused holidays, and travellers planning day trips to Hoi An. Marble Mountains are a 5-minute drive.

Non Nuoc Beach is measurably less crowded than My Khe and has calmer surf — better for families and those seeking space

Five-Star
Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort & Spa
📍 Non Nuoc Beach · 6-hectare compound

The defining Non Nuoc property. Six hectares, four terraced pools descending to the sand, a serious kids' programme, and room categories from garden-view standards to private pool villas. The beach here is wider and calmer than My Khe — swimmable and uncrowded outside peak season. Hoi An is 35 minutes south; Marble Mountains are a 5-minute drive.

💰 Budget tip

Hotels directly on My Khe command a 35–45% premium over properties one block back. In practice, the walk to the sand from a street-behind property is 60–120 seconds. For budget and mid-range travellers, the price gap rarely justifies the beachfront premium — especially in the An Thuong zone where the street itself is the destination.

Booking Guide

Beach Hotel Booking Tips

Book at least 6–8 weeks ahead for July–August. Da Nang's peak beach season coincides with Vietnamese school holidays and significant domestic tourism. Beachfront properties on My Khe sell out weeks in advance during this period, and rates spike significantly — often 40–60% above shoulder-season levels.

March through June is the sweet spot. The beach is at its best — calm water, consistent sunshine, low rainfall — and hotel rates haven't reached peak-season levels. This is when the best properties are available at reasonable prices. See the seasonal guide for monthly detail.

My Khe vs Non Nuoc: the honest comparison. My Khe wins on convenience, dining options, and urban access. Non Nuoc wins on space, calm water, and resort scale. For most first-time visitors, My Khe — specifically the central strip near An Thuong — is the right default. For families with young children, Non Nuoc is worth the extra 15-minute drive from the city.

🌊 Beach season note

Da Nang's northeast monsoon (September–November) makes beach swimming genuinely dangerous at times — red flags are taken seriously. The beachfront hotels remain open but redirect guests to pools. If your trip overlaps with this period, consider the InterContinental's sheltered private bay on Son Tra, which remains swimmable year-round.

FAQ

Beach Hotel FAQs

Is My Khe Beach or Non Nuoc Beach better?
It depends on your travel style. My Khe is better for urban access, restaurant variety, and convenience — the An Thuong village strip has some of Da Nang's best independent dining within walking distance. Non Nuoc is better for families (calmer water, larger resorts with kids' programmes), resort-focused stays where you won't explore the city much, and travellers planning frequent Hoi An day trips. Neither beach is significantly better in terms of sand quality — the difference is in atmosphere and surrounding infrastructure.
What is the best budget beach hotel in Da Nang?
The Pearl Hotel Da Nang on the southern My Khe stretch (Pham Van Dong area) is consistently the best-value beach-adjacent property. It's a 24-room French-colonial-influenced boutique from $48/night with genuinely good breakfast and a courtyard garden. The Horizon Grand Hotel in An Thuong ($65) is one block from My Khe and the best value for travellers who want boutique character with beach access.
Can you swim at Da Nang beach year-round?
The main beach swimming season is March through August. September through November brings the northeast monsoon — seas can be rough and red flags are common. December through February has improving but still variable conditions. The InterContinental Sun Peninsula's private bay on Son Tra is sheltered from the open sea and swimmable year-round, making it the best luxury option if you're travelling in the shoulder or off season.
Are there beachfront hostels or budget hotels in Da Nang?
Yes — the streets immediately behind My Khe Beach have a range of guesthouses and budget hotels from around $20–$40/night. Direct beachfront properties at this price point are rare, but a 2–5 minute walk to the sand is entirely achievable. The Pham Van Dong and Vo Nguyen Giap streets running parallel to the beach are the best areas to search.
