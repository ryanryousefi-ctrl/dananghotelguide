Da Nang's two beach zones, explained

Da Nang's coastline runs roughly north-to-south along the western edge of the South China Sea, with the main city sitting behind a natural curve of beach that's protected from the strongest open-ocean conditions. The result is a beach environment that's genuinely swimmable â€” not just photographable â€” for the better part of eight months a year.

My Khe Beach is the primary zone: 10 kilometres of wide, firm sand from Son Tra Peninsula's base down to the Marble Mountains foothills. The northern section near the An Thuong neighbourhood has the highest hotel density and the best independent restaurant scene. The southern section near Pham Van Dong is quieter, with a handful of boutique properties. Most travellers who say they're staying "at the beach" in Da Nang are on My Khe.

Non Nuoc Beach begins where My Khe ends, running south toward Hoi An. It's less developed, broader, and quieter â€” dominated by larger resort compounds rather than a mix of hotel types. The Marble Mountains sit immediately behind the beach, making Non Nuoc the only area where you can be at a luxury resort and walk to a UNESCO-adjacent cultural site in 15 minutes. Beach season & swimming conditions

Da Nang's beach season runs from approximately March through August â€” warm water, calm conditions, and reliably good swimming. The northeast monsoon brings rough surf and occasional flooding from September through November, during which beach swimming becomes inadvisable. December through February is variable: overcast and cool by Vietnamese standards, but swimmable on good days.
