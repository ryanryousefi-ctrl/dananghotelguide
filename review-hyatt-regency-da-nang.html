Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa Review

From $180/night ★ 8.8 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

The Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa sits on Non Nuoc Beach in the southern Ngu Hanh Son district, about 12 kilometres from Da Nang city centre. It is one of the most consistently recommended luxury beach resorts in Da Nang, occupying a substantial beachfront plot with five outdoor pools, a full-service spa, multiple restaurants, and 196 rooms and residences. Opened in 2011, the property has matured well and typically earns strong ratings across booking platforms.

Relative to comparable properties in Phuket, Bali, or the Maldives, the Hyatt Regency Danang represents good value. Its rates are competitive for what is delivered — five-star infrastructure, direct beach access, and a polished service culture trained to Hyatt's international standards. World of Hyatt points are earned and redeemable here, which makes it attractive for loyalty programme members.

Location

Non Nuoc Beach is one of Da Nang's quieter southern stretches, away from the busier My Khe Beach strip. The beach here is long, clean, and generally less crowded than the sections directly in front of Da Nang city. The Marble Mountains — a cluster of limestone hills with Buddhist pagodas and cave sanctuaries — are about 2 kilometres away and worth a half-day visit.

The 12-kilometre distance from the city centre means Dragon Bridge, Han Market, and the bulk of Da Nang's street food and nightlife options require a taxi. The drive typically takes 20–25 minutes in normal traffic. Hoi An is approximately 25 kilometres south, around 35 minutes by taxi — making the Hyatt a feasible base for day trips to both Da Nang city and Hoi An.

  • Airport: 14 km / 20 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 22 min taxi
  • Marble Mountains: 5 min taxi
  • Hoi An: 35 min by car
  • Non Nuoc beach: direct access

Rooms and Design

The 196 rooms and suites are spacious by any standard — entry-level Regency rooms start at around 52 square metres. Design is contemporary with Vietnamese craft elements, natural tones, and large balconies overlooking either the pool, garden, or ocean. The best rooms are the Premier Ocean View options on higher floors, which offer unobstructed South China Sea views.

The resort also has a collection of one- to three-bedroom Residences with full kitchens and living spaces — popular with families staying a week or more. Rooms are well-maintained and consistently rated highly for cleanliness. Bathrooms are large, with freestanding tubs in many categories.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

Five outdoor pools spread across the resort grounds are a highlight: a large lagoon-style family pool, a quieter adults-only pool, a beachfront pool, a children's pool, and a rooftop pool with panoramic views. The pool complex is one of the most extensive at any Da Nang hotel, and guest reviews consistently praise it as a standout feature.

The Serenity Spa is well-equipped with a full treatment menu, steam rooms, and relaxation facilities. A fitness centre, tennis courts, watersports, kids' club, and beach volleyball court complete the amenities package. Direct access to Non Nuoc Beach is clean and well-managed, with full beach service.

Dining Options

The resort's main restaurant, Cafe on the Park, operates an international buffet for breakfast and à la carte service through the day. The Viet Kitchen offers Vietnamese specialities in a more casual setting. Market 35 is a poolside bar and grill popular for lunch. The rooftop Bar35 serves cocktails and lighter fare with views over the beach.

Food quality across the outlets receives solid reviews — not at the level of Da Nang's best standalone restaurants, but appropriate for a resort context. Breakfast quality is consistently praised. Room service is available around the clock.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): $180–$250/night
  • Shoulder season: $250–$340/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $320–$480/night
  • Residences: from $350/night for one-bedroom

Who This Hotel Is Best For

The Hyatt Regency Danang appeals most strongly to families looking for a self-contained resort holiday, couples who want reliable five-star facilities, and World of Hyatt loyalty programme members. The multiple pools, beachfront access, and range of on-site dining options make it well-suited for guests who want to spend the majority of their time on-property. It also works well for those splitting time between the resort and day trips to Hoi An.

Pros
  • Five outdoor pools including adults-only and rooftop options
  • Genuine beachfront access on quieter Non Nuoc Beach
  • Well-maintained, spacious rooms with strong views
  • Marble Mountains nearby for easy cultural excursions
  • Hyatt World loyalty points earned
  • Good value against comparable Phuket or Bali resorts
Cons
  • 12 km from Da Nang city centre — daily taxis required
  • On-site dining is adequate but not exceptional
  • Pool area can get busy during Vietnamese holidays
  • No shuttle to Hoi An (third-party taxi required)

Final Verdict

The Hyatt Regency Danang is one of Da Nang's most complete resort packages — it covers the basics exceptionally well, offers one of the city's best pool complexes, and sits on a genuine beachfront plot in a quieter part of the coast. For families or couples prioritising a self-contained beach holiday over city access, it is a strong and reliable choice at a price point that undercuts comparable Southeast Asian resort markets. World of Hyatt members should factor in the points earning potential.

Is the Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa Worth It?

Yes, particularly for families and couples who want resort facilities without the extreme price premium of the InterContinental. The Hyatt Regency is one of the best all-round resort hotels on My Khe Beach — the pool complex is outstanding, the beach access is direct, and the room quality is consistently high. Non-Nuoc Beach at this southern stretch of My Khe is also somewhat quieter than the busier northern sections near the Hilton and Novotel. The main caveat is that it is a large resort and can feel busy in peak season, and the distance from Da Nang's city-centre restaurants requires planning. For a genuine beach resort holiday in central Vietnam, it ranks among the top three properties in the city.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • Non-Nuoc Beach — On-site. Direct access to Da Nang's quietest and widest stretch of My Khe Beach.
  • Marble Mountains — 5 min taxi. The resort's closest major attraction — five limestone formations with Buddhist temples.
  • My Khe Beach strip — 15 min taxi north. The lively main beach strip with hundreds of seafood restaurants and beach bars.
  • Dragon Bridge — 20 min taxi. Iconic fire-breathing bridge — best visited on Friday or Saturday night.
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — 25 min by car. UNESCO World Heritage site and the essential Da Nang day trip.
  • Da Nang Airport — 15 min taxi. Convenient transfer for early arrivals or late departures.
