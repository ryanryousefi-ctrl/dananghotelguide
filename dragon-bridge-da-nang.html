Dragon Bridge Da Nang: Fire & Water Show Guide 2026

By Ryan Yousefi Updated March 2026 🔥 Show: Fri, Sat & Sun · 9 PM Free to watch

Dragon Bridge — Cầu Rồng in Vietnamese — is Da Nang's most-photographed landmark and the centrepiece of the city's waterfront. The 666-metre bridge takes the form of a dragon rising from the Han River, built to symbolise good fortune and the power of the sea. On weekend evenings, the dragon comes to life: fire shoots from its mouth, water jets from beneath its belly, and the Han River riverfront fills with thousands of spectators.

This guide covers exactly when the show happens, where to position yourself for the best view, what to do in the surrounding area before and after, and which hotels give you the most convenient access.

Quick answer: The Dragon Bridge breathes fire every Friday, Saturday, and Sunday at 9:00 PM. The show lasts approximately 15 minutes. It is completely free to watch. On major Vietnamese public holidays, additional shows are sometimes scheduled.

When Does Dragon Bridge Breathe Fire?

The fire and water show runs on a fixed weekly schedule. There is no ticket required and no reserved viewing area — the entire riverfront becomes a public event space on show nights.

Day Show Time Duration Notes
Friday 9:00 PM ~15 minutes Regular weekly show
Saturday 9:00 PM ~15 minutes Regular weekly show
Sunday 9:00 PM ~15 minutes Regular weekly show
Public Holidays 9:00 PM ~15 minutes Additional shows sometimes added; check local announcements
Monday–Thursday — No show Bridge lit with LED displays only

How the Show Works

The performance consists of two distinct sequences:

  1. Fire sequence: Sustained bursts of flame erupt from the dragon's mouth, accompanied by light effects along the bridge structure. The flames are visible from a considerable distance across the river.
  2. Water spray sequence: High-pressure water jets shoot from beneath the dragon's head and along the bridge's lower section. Anyone standing directly in front of the dragon's mouth will get wet — this is expected and part of the appeal for many visitors.

Weather and Cancellations

Light rain does not cancel the show. During Da Nang's wet season (October–November in particular), there is a higher chance of storms that may delay or cancel the performance. There is no official real-time cancellation announcement system. The practical approach: head to the riverfront around 8:30 PM and observe crowd buildup — if the show is running, you'll see it clearly.

Holiday Schedule

On Tết (Vietnamese Lunar New Year), National Day (2 September), and Liberation Day (30 April), additional performances are sometimes added or the show is extended. Crowds during these holidays are significantly larger than a typical weekend. Arrive at least an hour early if you're visiting during a major holiday period.

Best Places to Watch the Show

From On Top of the Bridge (Under the Dragon)

The elevated walkway along the bridge offers the most dramatic perspective: you're essentially standing beneath the dragon while it breathes fire overhead. This is the most coveted viewing position and the one that fills up earliest.

Arrive early. If you want a spot on the bridge's upper walkway directly beneath the dragon's head, arrive at least 45–60 minutes before the 9 PM show. On holiday weekends, 90 minutes is not excessive. Spots fill from the head end inward — if you arrive 15 minutes before the show, you may only find space near the tail.

Access: Stairways to the pedestrian walkway are located at both the Hai Chau (west) and Son Tra (east) ends of the bridge. The west-bank stairs off Tran Hung Dao Street are the most commonly used. Once on the walkway, you can walk along both sides of the bridge.

What to expect: A dense crowd, a lot of phones raised, considerable noise, and — when the dragon fires — genuine heat from the flames if you're standing close. The water spray from the jets reaches the front rows of the walkway. If you don't want to get wet, position yourself further back along the walkway or to the side.

Watching from Street Level — Tran Hung Dao Street

Street-level viewing along Tran Hung Dao Street (the west bank promenade) is the easiest, most comfortable option for most visitors. The pavement is wide, there are benches, and dozens of vendors set up on show nights selling cold drinks, coconut water, corn on the cob, and grilled snacks.

You can spread out along several hundred metres of river frontage. There's no bad spot — the dragon is large enough to be visible from a wide arc. If you prefer comfort over intensity, grab a seat at one of the riverside cafés along this strip, order something cold, and watch the show from a café chair. Many cafés understand the economics of show night and don't pressure you to over-order.

Riverside Cafés and Rooftop Bars

Several venues along the river have terraces with clear sightlines to the bridge. Rooftop bars on the west bank offer the closest elevated views without the bridge crowd. Expect drink prices to be slightly higher than usual on show nights, and book a table in advance if you want guaranteed seating at a named venue on any show night.

River Cruise Boats

Watching from the water gives a completely different perspective — the dragon's reflection on the river during the fire sequence is one of the most photogenic views in Da Nang. Han River night cruises run specifically to catch the weekend show. See our companion guide: Han River Night Cruises — Dragon Bridge Show Guide 2026.

From Han River Bridges

The Han River Bridge (Cầu Sông Hàn), located immediately south of Dragon Bridge, is another free viewing option. The distance means you get a full-length view of Dragon Bridge rather than a close-up — good for photography. The Thuan Phuoc Bridge to the north offers a wider-angle view but is further away.

Son Tra Night Market

Directly across the bridge on the Son Tra (east) side, a small night market operates most evenings and swells considerably on show nights. It is not a large or sophisticated market — think a Vietnamese neighbourhood market with some tourist catering — but it's worth 30–45 minutes of your time before the show.

Bargaining expectations: On souvenir items, the first price quoted is rarely the final price. A polite counter-offer of 50–60% of the opening price is normal and not considered rude. On food items, prices are generally fixed and not subject to negotiation. Don't bargain aggressively over items worth a few thousand VND — it wastes everyone's time and creates bad feeling.

Surrounding Area

Tran Hung Dao Promenade

The riverside promenade along Tran Hung Dao Street is Da Nang's main evening social space. Locals walk, cycle, and gather here every night regardless of whether the bridge show is running. The atmosphere on show nights is noticeably electric — street food carts multiply, families arrive with picnic gear, and the air smells of grilled corn and sunscreen.

Love Lock Bridge (Cầu Tình Yêu)

A short walk south along the riverfront, this pedestrian bridge is decorated with thousands of padlocks left by couples. It is particularly photogenic around dusk. Access is free.

Han River Walking Paths

Both banks of the Han River have pedestrian paths extending several kilometres north and south. Cycling is popular here in the evenings; rental bicycles and e-scooters are available near the bridge from around 100,000–150,000 VND per hour.

Rooftop Bars Near Dragon Bridge

Several hotels and standalone rooftop venues sit within a short walk of the bridge. They range from casual to upscale. Most have clear sightlines to the bridge from their terraces and represent the most comfortable way to watch the show with a drink in hand. Reservation is strongly recommended on show nights (Friday, Saturday, and Sunday).

Hotels Near Dragon Bridge

The dragon's head is on the east (Son Tra) bank — that's where the fire and water come out, where the crowd gathers on the bridge, and where the Son Tra Night Market sits. If you want to step out of your hotel and be at the action in under 5 minutes, you need to be on this side. The options below are all within a short walk of the bridge head and the night market. Budget
Son Tra District Guesthouses
2–5 min walk

The streets immediately east of Dragon Bridge — particularly around Vo Van Kiet Street and the lanes running off it toward the night market — have a growing number of small guesthouses and budget hotels. Most were built or renovated in the last few years to serve the show-night visitor trade. Quality varies significantly; check recent reviews. Expect 250,000–450,000 VND per night for a private room with air conditioning. The walk to the dragon's head and the night market entrance is 2–5 minutes from most.

What to Bring

  • Water: Vendors sell cold drinks but at a premium on show nights. Bring your own if you're on a budget.
  • Light clothing: Da Nang evenings are warm most of the year. A light layer is sufficient — even in "winter" (December–February), evenings rarely drop below 20°C.
  • Camera or phone with a stable shooting mode: The fire sequence is bright enough for phone cameras to handle well. Video is often more satisfying than stills.
  • Small towel or rain jacket: If you're watching from the bridge walkway near the dragon's head, the water spray will reach you. This is not hypothetical — it gets people wet.
  • Cash in VND: Street food vendors and night market stalls rarely accept card. Bring small denominations. 200,000–300,000 VND (roughly $8–12 USD) covers snacks, drinks, and a souvenir or two.
  • Sun protection if arriving early: If you're grabbing a bridge spot an hour early and it's summer, the late-afternoon sun on the bridge is intense. Sunscreen or a hat makes the wait significantly more comfortable.
  • Patience for crowds: Show nights are genuinely busy. If you're sensitive to crowds, street-level or riverside café viewing is a better option than the bridge walkway.

Frequently Asked Questions

Dragon Bridge — Common Questions

What time does Dragon Bridge breathe fire?

The show runs every Friday, Saturday, and Sunday at 9:00 PM and lasts approximately 15 minutes. On major Vietnamese public holidays, additional performances are sometimes added — check local announcements if visiting during Tết, National Day (2 September), or Liberation Day (30 April).

Is the Dragon Bridge fire show free?

Yes — completely free. There is no admission charge to watch from the bridge walkway, the promenade, or anywhere along the riverfront. You only spend money if you choose to eat, drink, or shop at the surrounding vendors.

Does the dragon breathe fire every night?

No. The fire and water show runs on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday evenings at 9:00 PM, plus selected public holidays. On Monday through Thursday, the bridge is lit with colourful LED displays but there is no fire or water performance.

Can you stand on the bridge during the show?

Yes. The pedestrian walkways on both sides of the bridge are accessible via stairs at each end. For the best elevated view beneath the dragon's head, use the stairways off Tran Hung Dao Street on the west bank. Arrive at least 45–60 minutes early on weekend nights — the prime spots fill well before 9 PM. Be prepared: standing directly beneath the dragon's mouth means real heat from the flames and a genuine chance of getting wet from the water spray.

Is the Son Tra Night Market worth visiting?

Yes, for a casual 30–45 minutes. The market sells souvenirs, grilled seafood, fresh coconuts, lanterns, and local snacks. Prices on food are reasonable; souvenir prices are tourist-facing and require negotiation. Arriving before the show (around 8:00–8:30 PM) is easier than going after, when the post-show crowd converges on the market simultaneously.

Where should I stay to be closest to Dragon Bridge?

The closest hotels to the dragon's head and the Son Tra Night Market are on the east (Son Tra) bank. Calix Hotel is approximately 350m from the bridge head — around a 4-minute walk to the fire show and the night market entrance on Vo Van Kiet Street. Budget guesthouses cluster on the streets immediately east of the bridge. See our Best Hotels in Da Nang guide for a full city-wide breakdown.

What happens if it rains on show night?

Light rain does not typically cancel the show. Heavy storms or strong winds may cause a delay or cancellation. There is no official real-time cancellation feed. The practical approach: head to the riverfront around 8:30 PM — crowd buildup is the clearest signal that the show will proceed.

Is Dragon Bridge easy to reach from My Khe Beach?

Yes. Dragon Bridge is approximately a 10–15 minute drive from the My Khe Beach hotel strip. A Grab (ride-hailing) taxi is the most convenient option in the evening. On show nights, expect some congestion near the bridge from about 8:30 PM onward — ask the driver to drop you a few blocks away and walk the remainder.
