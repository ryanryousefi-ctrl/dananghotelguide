My Khe Beach — Best for Most Visitors

The default recommendation for first-time visitors

My Khe Beach is Da Nang's main tourist corridor — an 8-kilometre stretch of white sand running directly along the South China Sea. The Pham Van Dong beachfront strip is lined with mid-range and 4-star hotels, all with direct beach access and a walkable cluster of restaurants, cafes, and convenience stores. It's the most practical base for a Da Nang holiday.

The An Thuong neighbourhood sits behind the beach strip and adds a social layer: Da Nang's best cafe culture, a Saturday night market, and the highest concentration of expat-friendly bars. Most visitors never need to leave this kilometre-square zone.

🏖Direct sand access from almost every hotel
🍜100+ restaurants within a 5-minute walk
🌅West-facing for dramatic sunset views over the mountains
🚕10 minutes to Dragon Bridge, 15 to the Marble Mountains
💰Prices from $55/night (mid-range) to $200/night (4-star beachfront)

🌉 Best for City Lovers

Han River & Dragon Bridge — Best for Nightlife

City views, walkable bars, and better value than the beach strip

Staying near the Han River puts you in the pulse of Da Nang's urban energy. Dragon Bridge — the city's fire-breathing icon — is walking distance from most river hotels. The waterfront promenade is Da Nang's social hub: evening markets, street food stalls, and the best rooftop bar scene in the city.

Hotels here are generally 15–25% cheaper than equivalent My Khe properties. The trade-off: you're 3–5 km from the beach (a 10-minute taxi). For travellers who want to explore the city, visit night markets, or make day trips to Hoi An, this is the most practical location.

🐉Dragon Bridge walking distance — fire show on Saturdays at 21:00
🍺Best nightlife access: Sky 36, Buddha Bar, Rong Den Beach Club
🛒Han Market, Con Market, and night markets all nearby
💰Typically 20–30% cheaper than equivalent beach hotels

🌿 Most Exclusive Area

Son Tra Peninsula — Best for Luxury Resorts

Jungle cliffs, ocean views, and total seclusion

Son Tra is a protected jungle peninsula jutting into the South China Sea, 8 km north of the city. It's dramatically different from every other part of Da Nang — steep mountain roads, wild monkey populations, and clifftop infinity pools with uninterrupted bay views. This is where Da Nang's flagship luxury resort is located.

The InterContinental Danang Sun Peninsula Resort defines this area — 197 villas on a private clifftop, a MICHELIN-starred restaurant, and Asia's longest non-urban funicular. It's one of the most dramatic resort settings in Southeast Asia. The peninsula also hosts smaller boutique properties for those who want nature without the five-star price.

🌊Private clifftop villas above the South China Sea
🦁Protected jungle — red-shanked douc langur monkeys on-site
⭐MICHELIN-starred dining at the InterContinental
🚁Isolated setting — taxi to city takes 20–25 minutes
💰From $350/night at the InterContinental; boutique options from $120

🏙 Best Value Area

Central Da Nang — Best for Budget Hotels

Local flavour, affordable rates, and easy day-trip access

Central Da Nang — particularly the Hai Chau and Thanh Khe districts — is where most locals live and where prices are consistently the lowest. Budget hotels, family-run guesthouses, and mid-range business hotels cluster in a grid of walkable streets near local markets and street food alleys. It's the most authentic part of the city for travellers who want to avoid tourist bubble pricing.

The trade-off is that you're 4–6 km from the beach — a 10–15 minute taxi. But if your priority is budget, local food, and easy access to the Marble Mountains and Hoi An day trips, this area delivers the best overall value in the city.

🛒Con Market and Han Market — authentic local shopping
🍜Best street food prices in the city — Mi Quang, Banh Mi, Bun Cha Ca
💰Hotels from $20–$65/night
🗺Best base for Marble Mountains, Hoi An, and Hue day trips
🏖10–15 minutes to My Khe Beach by taxi

Seasonal pricing note: Da Nang peaks in July–August (summer school holidays) when beach hotel rates rise 25–40%. Common Questions

Da Nang Hotel Location FAQ

Where is the best area to stay in Da Nang for first-time visitors?

My Khe Beach is the best area for first-time visitors to Da Nang. The Pham Van Dong beachfront strip offers direct beach access, a high density of restaurants and cafes within walking distance, and hotels across all budgets from $55/night. It's the most practical and enjoyable base for a first trip. See our full neighbourhood guide for detailed area comparisons.

How far are Da Nang beach hotels from the city centre and Dragon Bridge?

My Khe Beach hotels are 3–5 km from Da Nang city centre and Dragon Bridge. In light traffic, this takes 8–12 minutes by taxi (typically 40,000–60,000 VND). Most visitors find this distance very easy — taxis and Grab cars are plentiful, and many hotels also offer free shuttle services to and from the city.

Is Son Tra Peninsula far from Da Nang city?

Son Tra Peninsula is 8–10 km from Da Nang city centre — a 20–25 minute drive by taxi. The InterContinental provides a shuttle service for guests. The isolation is part of the appeal: Son Tra feels worlds away from city noise, with jungle roads, wildlife, and dramatic ocean views. It's best suited to travellers who want a true resort experience rather than city access.

What is the cheapest area to stay in Da Nang?

Central Da Nang — particularly the Hai Chau and Thanh Khe districts near Han Market — has the cheapest hotels, starting from $20–$35/night for clean budget guesthouses. You sacrifice beach proximity (10–15 minutes by taxi) but gain access to authentic local food and the lowest prices in the city. See our Da Nang budget guide for specific recommendations.

Which Da Nang hotels are closest to Dragon Bridge?

The closest hotels to Dragon Bridge are on the Han River strip — the Novotel Danang Premier Han River and Brilliant Hotel are both within a 5-minute walk. The bridge's fire-breathing show takes place every Saturday and Sunday at 21:00 and is best viewed from the riverfront promenade. See the hotel reviews page for full listings.

Are there luxury hotels on My Khe Beach?

Yes — My Khe Beach has several 4-star and upscale 5-star options, including the Hyatt Regency Da Nang (at the quieter My An stretch), the Sheraton Grand, and Pullman. However, Da Nang's flagship luxury resort — the InterContinental Sun Peninsula — is on Son Tra Peninsula, not My Khe. See the full luxury hotels in Da Nang guide for the best options by area.
