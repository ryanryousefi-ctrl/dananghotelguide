Before You Arrive

Da Nang in Three Days

Da Nang from above — 10km of beach to the west, Son Tra Peninsula to the north, and Marble Mountains visible on the southern horizon

Three days is enough to see Da Nang's highlights without rushing, and short enough that you can move at a genuine tourist pace rather than burning through attractions. The city is compact — My Khe Beach, the Old Quarter streets, Dragon Bridge, and the Marble Mountains are all within 20 minutes of each other by Grab.

The structure below allocates: Day 1 to the beach and Han River district (your arrival day if flying in the morning), Day 2 to the Marble Mountains and Son Tra Peninsula, and Day 3 to Hoi An — 30km south, one of Southeast Asia's best-preserved trading towns and comfortably done as a day trip. If you have a fourth day, Ba Na Hills (the French-colonial hillstation with the Golden Bridge) fits cleanly between Days 2 and 3.

When to visit: This itinerary works best between February and August, when Da Nang's dry season produces reliable beach weather. September through November brings the northeast monsoon — Hoi An floods regularly in October and the Marble Mountains can be slippery. See the full weather guide for month-by-month conditions.

Day 1

Day 1 — Beach, City & River

An arrival-day structure that doesn't require early starts: beach in the morning, city exploration in the afternoon, and Dragon Bridge at night.

My Khe Beach — 10km of white sand, best swum between 6am and 10am before the afternoon wind picks up

Morning — My Khe Beach (6am–10am). Check in to your hotel and go straight to the beach. My Khe is at its best early — the water is calm before the afternoon onshore wind, the sand is uncrowded, and the light is good. Most hotels on the strip have direct beach access. The 2km stretch between the Sheraton and the Hyatt Regency is the cleanest section. Eat breakfast at one of the streetside cà phê (Vietnamese iced coffee) carts on Võ Nguyên Giáp — the main beach road.

Afternoon — Hải Châu District and Hàn Market (1pm–5pm). Grab a taxi to Hàn Market (Chợ Hàn) — Da Nang's central covered market and the best place to buy fabric, lacquerware, and local snacks. Allow an hour. Then walk the riverside promenade along Bạch Đằng Street — this is Da Nang's most photogenic urban strip, with colonial-era buildings, the Trần Thị Lý Bridge, and cafés facing the Han River. If you have time before dinner, the Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture on Trưng Nữ Vương Street is a 45-minute visit and houses the world's largest collection of Cham artefacts.

Evening — Dragon Bridge & Han River Dining (6pm onwards). Dragon Bridge — the bridge shaped like a full-scale dragon spanning the Han River — breathes fire and water every Saturday and Sunday at 9pm. Arrive at Waterfront Restaurant on Bạch Đằng Street by 7pm for river-terrace dinner and a direct view of the bridge. The promenade is crowded on weekends; arrive early to secure a table. If you're visiting mid-week, the bridge is still worth seeing lit at night from the riverside, and dinner on Bạch Đằng remains excellent without the crowds.

🚖 Transport Day 1

Airport to hotel: fixed-rate taxi (roughly $6–8) or Grab (usually $4–5). All Day 1 stops are within a 20-minute taxi or Grab ride of My Khe Beach hotels. The Grab app works throughout Da Nang — see the transport guide for pricing and tips.

Day 2

Day 2 — Marble Mountains & Son Tra

The two natural highlights within Da Nang's city limits — ancient limestone caves carved into active pagodas, and a forested peninsula with the best city panorama in Vietnam.

The Marble Mountains — five limestone karsts 9km south of the city, each named after a natural element, each containing Buddhist shrines and cave chambers

Morning — Marble Mountains (8am–11am). The Marble Mountains (Ngũ Hành Sơn) are five limestone karsts rising from the coastal plain 9km south of the city. The most visited is Thủy Sơn (Water Mountain), which contains two cave pagodas — Huyền Không Cave and Tàng Chơn Cave — carved into the mountain itself, with natural skylights filtering light onto ancient Buddha carvings. The summit viewpoint overlooks Non Nuoc Beach to the east and the Da Nang skyline to the north. Take the elevator up (20,000 VND) and walk down. Allow 2 hours minimum; bring water. Enter from the west side off Huyền Trân Công Chúa road.

Lunch — Non Nuoc Beach area (11am–1pm). Non Nuoc Beach, directly east of the Marble Mountains, is quieter than My Khe and good for a swim and lunch. The beach restaurants along the access road serve fresh seafood — the grilled whole fish with salt and chilli is reliably good at any of the open-air restaurants directly on the sand.

Afternoon — Son Tra Peninsula (2pm–6pm). Son Tra Peninsula juts into the sea 8km north of central Da Nang and rises to 696 metres. The Linh Ứng Pagoda — a 67-metre white Buddha statue visible from across the city — is the most visited site and genuinely impressive at close range. The summit road passes through primary forest and offers unobstructed views across Da Nang Bay and back to the Marble Mountains. Red-shanked doucs (a rare and striking primate) live in the forest; early morning or late afternoon are the best times to see them. The drive takes 45 minutes from the city by Grab.

Evening — An Thuong Village (7pm onwards). Return to the An Thuong neighbourhood behind My Khe Beach for dinner — this is the densest concentration of restaurants in the city, with Vietnamese, Japanese, Korean, and Western options within a few streets. The MICHELIN Bib Gourmand restaurants in the area (Madame Lan for bún bò Huế, Mì Quảng 1A for the city's signature noodle dish) are both within a 10-minute walk of the beach hotels. See the dining guide for specific recommendations.

📋 Day 2 logistics

Marble Mountains to Son Tra: Grab takes 35 minutes and costs around $5. You can also hire a motorbike ($8–12/day) and combine both stops in a single loop — Marble Mountains in the morning, back through the city, up Son Tra in the afternoon. See the transport guide for motorbike hire options and the Son Tra road conditions.

Day 3

Day 3 — Hoi An Day Trip

Hoi An Ancient Town — UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Southeast Asia's best-preserved trading ports — is 30km south and easily done as a full day trip from Da Nang.

Hoi An — the lanterns go up at dusk; arrive by 3pm to walk the Ancient Town before the evening light show begins

Getting there. Grab from Da Nang to Hoi An Ancient Town costs $12–15 and takes 45 minutes. Negotiate a fixed-price return with the driver (typically $20–24 for a return trip with a 5–6 hour wait) or book a round-trip taxi through your hotel. There is no direct bus service worth taking as a day tripper. Leave Da Nang by 8:30am to arrive before the tour groups.

Morning — Ancient Town (9am–12pm). The Ancient Town is a compact grid of 15th–19th century merchant houses, Chinese assembly halls, Japanese covered bridge, and tailoring shops, all set along the Thu Bồn River. Buy a combined entry ticket (120,000 VND) which gives access to five heritage sites of your choice — the Japanese Covered Bridge, Phùng Hưng Old House, Tấn Ký Old House, Quan Công Temple, and the Fujian Assembly Hall are the five most significant. Walk the old town streets before 11am to beat the heat and the crowds.

Lunch — Hoi An food scene (12pm–2pm). Hoi An has its own MICHELIN Bib Gourmand listings and a distinctive local cuisine: cơm gà Hội An (Hoi An chicken rice), bánh mì Phượng (the bánh mì widely cited as Vietnam's best), and cao lầu (thick noodles with pork and greens, made with water specifically from Hoi An's ancient wells — not reproducible elsewhere). Bánh Mì Phượng at 2B Phan Châu Trinh operates a 7am–6:30pm queue; arrive by noon.

Afternoon — An Bàng Beach or Cửa Đại Beach (2pm–5pm). Hoi An's beaches — An Bàng and Cửa Đại — are 5km from the Ancient Town by bicycle (hire for 50,000 VND/day from any guesthouse) or tuk-tuk. An Bàng is the better beach: less developed, cleaner water, and a row of beach-bar restaurants that are ideal for a late afternoon swim before the drive back to Da Nang.

Evening return to Da Nang. Return by 6pm to avoid evening traffic on the coastal road. If you arranged a waiting driver, confirm pickup from the Ancient Town entry gate. Dinner back in Da Nang — the An Thuong strip and Bạch Đằng riverside are 20 minutes from where most My Khe Beach hotels are located.

🗓 Optional: 4th day — Ba Na Hills

If you have a fourth day, Ba Na Hills — a French colonial mountain station at 1,500m with the famous Golden Bridge — is a logical extension. Cable car ascent takes 20 minutes from the base station. The complex is 25km west of Da Nang; a full visit needs 6–7 hours. Best visited on a weekday to avoid weekend domestic tourist crowds.

Practical Tips

Before You Go

Currency. Vietnam Dong (VND). ATMs are widely available on My Khe Beach road and throughout Hải Châu district. Most tourist restaurants accept USD and card. Street food and markets are cash-only. Daily budget: $40–60 covers accommodation (mid-range), three meals, and transport if you use Grab throughout.

SIM card. Buy a Viettel or Vietnamobile SIM at Da Nang Airport arrivals — 30-day data plans cost $5–8. Grab requires a local or registered SIM for payment in some configurations; having a local number avoids friction. See the digital nomad guide for SIM card options and carrier comparison.

Transport. Grab (Vietnam's Uber equivalent) is reliable, cheap, and works from the airport. Motorbike hire ($8–12/day) makes Day 2 significantly more flexible. Hoi An is best done with a pre-arranged driver rather than Grab, as surge pricing on the return can make it expensive. Full breakdown in the transport guide.

Weather. This itinerary works best in the dry season (February–August). Ocean swimming is safe year-round in the dry season; typhoon season (October–November) can bring closures. The weather by month guide covers sea conditions, rainfall, and the best window for each activity.

Where to Stay

Hotels for This Itinerary

For this three-day itinerary, My Khe Beach hotels give the best base — you're within walking distance of Day 1's beach morning, and 20–30 minutes by Grab from every other stop. The An Thuong restaurant strip is directly behind most My Khe properties.

The InterContinental Sun Peninsula on Son Tra Peninsula makes Day 2's visit seamless — you're already on-site for Linh Ứng Pagoda — but adds 20 minutes of transit to the Marble Mountains and city. See the luxury hotels guide for a full review.
