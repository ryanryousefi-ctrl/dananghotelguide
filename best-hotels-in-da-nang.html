Where to Stay in Da Nang

Da Nang divides across four distinct accommodation zones. Your choice shapes how your trip feels day-to-day more than any individual hotel decision.

The Han River divides Da Nang's beach zone from its urban restaurant and nightlife district

Da Nang's main hotel strip â€” 10 kilometres of beach with the densest concentration of restaurants, beach bars, and hotels centred around the An Thuong village area. Beach access is direct. Son Tra Peninsula

A protected nature reserve 20 minutes from the city, Son Tra is where Da Nang hides its most dramatic accommodation. Beach is 10â€“15 minutes by taxi from most properties. Best for business travellers and those using Da Nang as a base for day trips to Hoi An and Hue.

At the southern end of the My Khe coastline, Non Nuoc is measurably less crowded, with some of Da Nang's best resort properties â€” the Hyatt Regency, Platinum Retreat, and several large Vietnamese resort brands. Marble Mountains are within walking distance, and Hoi An is under 40 minutes south.

Frequently Asked Questions

What area is best to stay in Da Nang?
My Khe Beach is the best all-round base for most visitors. You're on the sand, within walking distance of the city's best independent restaurants, and 20 minutes from Hoi An. Son Tra Peninsula is right if you prioritise seclusion and resort atmosphere over urban convenience. The Han River strip suits business travellers and those who prefer a city base to a beach one.
Is it better to stay near My Khe Beach or Non Nuoc Beach?
For most leisure travellers, My Khe â€” specifically the central section near An Thuong village â€” gives the best combination of beach access, restaurants, and urban convenience. Non Nuoc is the better choice for families with young children (calmer water), resort-focused holidays where you don't plan to explore the city much, and travellers heading to Hoi An frequently. The Hyatt Regency and Platinum Retreat at Non Nuoc are among Da Nang's best resort properties.
Are there luxury resorts in Da Nang?
Yes â€” and more than most people expect. The InterContinental Sun Peninsula Resort on Son Tra Peninsula has consistently placed in CondÃ© Nast Traveler's global top-hotel rankings and is genuinely world-class. Other strong luxury options include the Sheraton Grand Danang, the Hyatt Regency Non Nuoc, and the Pullman. Rates at the luxury end typically run $180â€“$400 per night, significantly better value than comparable quality in the Maldives, coastal Thailand, or Bali.
How many days should you spend in Da Nang?
Three to five days is the right range for a standalone Da Nang trip. Three days covers the essentials: beach time, a day at Ba Na Hills or Marble Mountains, and an afternoon or evening in Hoi An. Five days allows you to slow down â€” a day at Non Nuoc, time on the Son Tra Peninsula, a food-focused evening in the city. For budget accommodation with direct beach proximity, look in the streets around Pham Van Dong and Truong Sa roads.
Is Da Nang good for Korean travellers?
Exceptionally so. Da Nang is one of Korea's most popular short-haul destinations, with direct flights from Seoul (Incheon), Busan, and Daegu. Korean-language menus are widespread in the An Thuong restaurant strip, and several hotels maintain Korean-speaking staff. The city's safety, cleanliness, short flight time (4â€“5 hours), and strong value make it a favourite for Korean families and couples.
