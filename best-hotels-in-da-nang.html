Where to Stay in Da Nang

Neighbourhood Breakdown

The area you choose determines your entire holiday experience. Here is a direct, honest breakdown of each zone. For more detail visit our full Where to Stay guide.

🌊
My Khe Beach
Best all-round neighbourhood

Da Nang's busiest and most complete beach zone. The 8km stretch of beachfront runs from the Son Tra Peninsula south toward My An. An Thuong village at its northern end is the social and culinary heart of tourist Da Nang. You walk to dinner. You walk to the beach. You walk to cafés. Nothing requires a taxi. For first-time visitors, this is the default and correct choice.

Who it suitsEveryone — especially first-timers
Airport transfer10–12 minutes
Hotel prices$70–$360 / night
Evening sceneExcellent — An Thuong bars & cafés
Beach qualityVery good — busy in peak season
🌞
My An Beach
Premium resort territory

Immediately south of My Khe, My An is where the large five-star resorts (Furama, Sheraton Grand, Fusion Maia, Premier Village) are concentrated. The beach is wider and cleaner than My Khe and significantly less crowded. The trade-off is convenience: you're dependent on taxis for restaurants and evening entertainment. Right for week-long stays at large resorts; wrong for city-curious travellers.

Who it suitsFamilies, resort stays, luxury seekers
Airport transfer15–18 minutes
Hotel prices$120–$560 / night
Evening sceneMostly in-resort dining
Beach qualityExcellent — wide & clean
🏛
Hai Chau / Han River
City culture & business

Hai Chau district is the administrative and cultural heart of Da Nang — Dragon Bridge, Han Market, the city's museums, and its best street-food alleys are all here. Hotels are cheaper than the beach zones, the transport links to Hoi An are superior, and the atmosphere is genuinely Vietnamese rather than tourist-oriented. Wrong for beach holidays; right for anyone who wants to understand the city.

Who it suitsBusiness travellers, city explorers
Airport transfer15–20 minutes
Hotel prices$50–$150 / night
Beach access15–20 min taxi to My Khe
Evening sceneGood — local restaurants & bars
🏭
Son Tra Peninsula
Ultra-luxury & seclusion

Son Tra is a protected wildlife reserve peninsula 15km northeast of the city. Development is restricted, which means only two luxury hotels operate here — the InterContinental and Radisson Blu. The views are dramatic, the beaches are private, and the sense of seclusion is complete. The trade-off is total dependence on hotel F&B and taxi trips for any off-resort activity. Recommended for honeymoons, anniversaries, and guests who actively want to disconnect.

Who it suitsLuxury couples, honeymoons
Airport transfer20–30 minutes
Hotel prices$160–$900+ / night
BeachPrivate bays — no public access
Evening sceneIn-resort only
🏛
Non Nuoc Beach
Quietest beach, golf & nature

Non Nuoc is Da Nang's southernmost beach zone, running from the Marble Mountains area toward Hoi An. The beach is wider and emptier than My Khe — genuinely deserted on weekday mornings. This is where the Hyatt Regency, Sandy Beach, and BRG Golf Resort sit. The access road (the Coastal Highway) now runs all the way to Hoi An, making day trips very straightforward. For guests who want the city's best hotels but can't stand crowds, Non Nuoc is the answer.

Who it suitsFamilies, golfers, quiet seekers
Airport transfer20–25 minutes

Common Questions

Da Nang Hotels FAQ

The questions we hear most from readers planning their first Da Nang trip.

Is it better to stay by the beach or the river in Da Nang?
For first-time visitors, the beach is the right call — specifically the My Khe strip. You are two minutes from the water, surrounded by Da Nang's best restaurants, and never need a taxi for an evening out. The riverfront (Han River / Hai Chau) suits business travellers and people who want easy access to Dragon Bridge, Han Market, and Hoi An day trips without paying beach-zone premiums. If you can only choose once: beach. The one exception is a business-focused trip of 2–3 nights where city access matters more than sand.
How much do hotels cost in Da Nang in 2026?
Da Nang has the widest price spread of any comparable beach destination in Southeast Asia. Expect to pay $18–35/night for a decent budget guesthouse, $55–110 for a solid 3–4 star property with a pool, $120–200 for quality beachfront with proper resort facilities, and $200–600+ for the true luxury tier (InterContinental, Hyatt Regency, Fusion Maia). Prices drop 25–40% outside peak season. High season runs November through February (cool, dry) and June through August (hot, sunny). The shoulder months of March–May are excellent: fewer crowds, lower prices, still good weather. See our Da Nang travel cost guide for a full budget breakdown.
When is the best time to visit Da Nang?
March through August is peak beach season — low humidity, sustained sunshine, calm South China Sea, and water temperatures around 28°C. The shoulder months of February and September offer excellent conditions with noticeably fewer crowds. November through January is the northeast monsoon: prices are lowest but rain is frequent and ocean swimming is rough. Avoid October if possible — it is statistically the wettest month (average 400mm rainfall). For a detailed month-by-month guide, see our best time to visit Da Nang guide.
Are Da Nang hotels safe?
Da Nang is one of Vietnam's safest cities and consistently ranks among Southeast Asia's safest destinations for solo travellers, including women travelling alone. Petty crime exists as in any tourist area but is genuinely rare by regional standards. Every reputable hotel on this list uses 24-hour security, in-room safes, and key-card access. The beach areas are well-lit and patrolled at night. As of 2026, Da Nang has had no significant security incidents affecting tourism and maintains its reputation as Vietnam's most liveable city.
Where do Korean tourists stay in Da Nang?
Korean visitors — the largest foreign tourist group in Da Nang — concentrate heavily in the My Khe Beach zone, particularly the northern stretch near An Thuong village. Properties like Wyndham Soleil, TMS Hotel Da Nang Beach, and A La Carte attract significant Korean clientele. The An Thuong neighbourhood has evolved into a Korean-friendly dining and café corridor, with Korean-language signage visible in many restaurant windows. Non Nuoc Beach (near the Marble Mountains) is the second preference for Korean group tours. Hotels with Korean-speaking front desk staff include Wyndham Soleil and several mid-range properties on An Thuong Street.
How far are Da Nang hotels from the airport?
Da Nang International Airport (DAD) is centrally located — one of the most conveniently positioned airports in Southeast Asia. My Khe Beach hotels: 10–12 minutes. My An Beach hotels: 15–18 minutes. Hai Chau city centre: 15–20 minutes. Non Nuoc Beach: 20–25 minutes. Son Tra Peninsula: 20–30 minutes. Grab taxi is reliable and inexpensive: expect to pay $4–8 for most hotel zones. Pre-arranged airport transfers from hotels typically cost $8–15 for the one-way trip. See our airport transfer guide for full logistics.
