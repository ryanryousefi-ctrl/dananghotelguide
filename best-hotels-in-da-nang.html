Best Luxury Beach Resorts

5 hotels

Da Nang's luxury tier is no longer a secret. The city now hosts properties from IHG, Hyatt, Marriott, and Fusion that genuinely compete with Bali and Phuket — at prices that run 25–40% lower. The five picks below set the bar.

1
★ Editor's #1 Pick

InterContinental Danang Sun Peninsula Resort

★★★★★
Son Tra Peninsula 25 min from airport · Private bay

The single most dramatic hotel setting in Vietnam. Bill Bensley's architecture cascades down a forested Son Tra hillside via a series of funiculars connecting five distinct levels — lagoon pool, beach club, restaurants, and villas. The MICHELIN-recognised CITRON restaurant alone justifies a night here. What separates the InterContinental from every other luxury property in Da Nang is the completeness of the experience: you don't need to leave the resort grounds for anything. The private beach is genuinely private, the pool terrace framing views of the South China Sea is one of the best in Southeast Asia, and the service-to-room ratio is exceptional.

🌞 Best for: Couples, honeymooners, anniversary trips, anyone seeking total immersion 🚶 Beach access: Private bay — no public beach access needed
Pros

  • MICHELIN-recognised CITRON restaurant

  • Funicular access throughout resort
  • Multiple pool and beach environments
Cons
  • Far from city restaurants & bars
  • Taxi required for any off-resort trips
  • Premium price for in-resort F&B

Best 5-Star Beach Value

Hyatt Regency Danang Resort & Spa

★★★★★
Non Nuoc Beach 20 min from airport · Direct beachfront

The Hyatt Regency is the most operationally polished large resort in Da Nang. Six pools, four restaurants, a 120-metre main pool with a lazy river, and direct access to Non Nuoc Beach — the quieter, wider stretch south of the city — make this the top choice for families and couples who want genuine five-star infrastructure without the price ceiling of the InterContinental. The 197 rooms and 63 villas give enough scale to always feel alive without feeling crowded. Club rooms unlock the rooftop lounge with complimentary sundowners; worth the upgrade.

Best for Families

Sheraton Grand Danang Resort

★★★★★
My An Beach 15 min from airport · Beachfront

The Sheraton Grand is Da Nang's premier family resort — and it's not particularly close. The 785-room property features an extraordinary water complex with waterslides, a lazy river, kids' splash areas, and a dedicated children's pool zone. The Grand Club Lounge is the best hotel lounge in Da Nang by some margin. Rooms are generous (standard rooms start at 46 sqm) and the My An Beach frontage is excellent: wide, clean, and calmer than My Khe. The sheer scale means there's always a poolside chair, a dinner option, or a kids' activity available. For a week-long family holiday, this is the benchmark.

Best Spa & Wellness

Fusion Maia Da Nang

★★★★★
My An Beach 15 min from airport · Beachfront

Fusion Maia is the only all-suite, all-inclusive spa resort in Da Nang — and one of the most genuinely distinctive hotels in Vietnam. Every suite has its own private plunge pool. The rate includes twice-daily spa treatments per person, breakfast served anywhere at any time, and no single-supplement surcharge. Best Location — My Khe

Pullman Danang Beach Resort

★★★★★
My Khe Beach 10 min from airport · Beachfront

The Pullman is the luxury hotel with the best location in Da Nang, full stop. Sitting directly on My Khe Beach's prime northern stretch — 10 minutes from the airport, walkable to An Thuong village's restaurants and bars — it offers five-star service without sacrificing access to the city. The 187-room property has a strong F&B operation (the rooftop bar is genuinely one of the best sundowner spots in the city), an excellent beach club, and rooms with real ocean views. If you want luxury and you also want to walk to dinner without a taxi, this is your hotel.

Best Mid-Range Beach Hotels

5 hotels

Da Nang's mid-range ($70–$160) is where the city earns its reputation for value. These five hotels offer beachfront access, pools, and solid service at prices that would buy you a formula hotel 2km from the beach in Phuket or Bali.

Best Value My Khe

A La Carte Da Nang Beach

★★★★☆
My Khe Beach 10 min from airport · Direct beachfront

A La Carte punches well above its price bracket. The rooftop infinity pool bar is one of the most photographed spots in Da Nang — sunrise over My Khe from the top-floor pool is spectacular — and the hotel is genuinely on the beach. Self-catering apartment-style rooms (with kitchenettes) make it particularly good value for longer stays or families who want flexibility. The breakfast buffet is strong, service is attentive without being intrusive, and the location in the heart of My Khe's best stretch is faultless. For $85–130 in mid-season, this is the best overall package on My Khe Beach.

Established Favourite

Furama Resort Da Nang

★★★★★
My An Beach 15 min from airport · Beachfront

Furama is the resort that put Da Nang on the international luxury map in the early 2000s — and despite newer competition, it remains one of the best-value five-star experiences in the city. The 198-villa property sits on a superb My An Beach frontage with lush tropical gardens, three pools, a full spa, and outstanding food and beverage. Furama's great strength is consistency: it has two decades of fine-tuned operations, and it shows. Rooms are large by Vietnamese standards (standard rooms from 54 sqm), the beach is excellent, and the service culture — genuinely warm — is something newer hotels are still learning. At $110–180, this is exceptional five-star value.

10
Quietest Beachfront

Sandy Beach Non Nuoc Resort

★★★★☆
Non Nuoc Beach 20 min from airport · Beachfront

If your priority is a genuinely quiet beach with no crowds, Sandy Beach Non Nuoc is the answer. Non Nuoc is the least-developed stretch of Da Nang's coastline — wider, sandier, and dramatically less busy than My Khe. Sandy Beach occupies an excellent position here with 159 rooms spread across low-rise villas set in manicured gardens. It lacks the height-driven views of My Khe towers but more than compensates with atmosphere, space, and tranquillity. The pool is excellent, the beach is wide and clean, and the overall pace is deliberately unhurried. Best for guests who actively don't want to be in the middle of the action.

Best Private Villa Experience

Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang

★★★★★
Non Nuoc Beach 20 min from airport · Beachfront

Vinpearl's Da Nang luxury property offers the best private-pool villa experience in the city outside the InterContinental. The 165 standalone villas are set in sprawling grounds directly on Non Nuoc Beach, each with its own pool terrace, outdoor shower, and privacy screening. The design is unapologetically Vietnamese-luxury — warm timber tones, carved stone, tropical planting — and it works. The beach is excellent, the main pool complex is substantial, and the two restaurants are better than the property's relative obscurity suggests. For privacy-first travellers at the $150–280 tier, this is the best call in Da Nang.

Best Riverfront Hotels

4 hotels

Da Nang's Han River is the city's second landmark after the beach — Dragon Bridge, the Tran Thi Ly Bridge, and the glittering riverside are best experienced from a hotel room facing the water. These properties suit business travellers, city explorers, and anyone visiting for a few nights rather than a full beach holiday.

15
Best River View Hotel

Novotel Danang Premier Han River

★★★★☆
Han River / Hai Chau 15 min from airport · Riverside

The Novotel Han River is the most satisfying riverfront hotel in Da Nang. From the 8th floor upwards, river-facing rooms offer a direct view of Dragon Bridge — including the Saturday and Sunday night fire-breathing display — that no other hotel in the city can match. The property is located in the heart of Hai Chau district, walkable to Han Market, the Dragon Bridge, and Da Nang's museum cluster. At $85–130, it's excellent value for the location and level of operation. Business travellers particularly appreciate the high-floor rooms: working from the desk with Dragon Bridge in the window is a genuinely memorable experience.

🌞 Best for: Business travellers, city explorers, short stays, Dragon Bridge viewers 🚶 Beach access: 10-minute taxi / 30-minute walk to My Khe Beach

  • Excellent value for quality & location
  • Easy Hoi An day trip logistics
  • Strong breakfast included rates
Cons
  • No direct beach access
  • Pool is small and on upper floor
  • Street noise on lower city-facing rooms

Best for Points Redemptions

★★★★☆

Category 4–5 properties in Da Nang consistently represent some of the programme's best beach redemptions.

Common Questions

Da Nang Hotels FAQ

The questions we hear most from readers planning their first Da Nang trip.

Is it better to stay by the beach or the river in Da Nang?
For first-time visitors, the beach is the right call — specifically the My Khe strip. You are two minutes from the water, surrounded by Da Nang's best restaurants, and never need a taxi for an evening out. The riverfront (Han River / Hai Chau) suits business travellers and people who want easy access to Dragon Bridge, Han Market, and Hoi An day trips without paying beach-zone premiums. If you can only choose once: beach. The one exception is a business-focused trip of 2–3 nights where city access matters more than sand.
How much do hotels cost in Da Nang in 2026?
Da Nang has the widest price spread of any comparable beach destination in Southeast Asia. Expect to pay $18–35/night for a decent budget guesthouse, $55–110 for a solid 3–4 star property with a pool, $120–200 for quality beachfront with proper resort facilities, and $200–600+ for the true luxury tier (InterContinental, Hyatt Regency, Fusion Maia). Prices drop 25–40% outside peak season. High season runs November through February (cool, dry) and June through August (hot, sunny). The shoulder months of March–May are excellent: fewer crowds, lower prices, still good weather. See our Da Nang travel cost guide for a full budget breakdown.
When is the best time to visit Da Nang?
March through August is peak beach season — low humidity, sustained sunshine, calm South China Sea, and water temperatures around 28°C. The shoulder months of February and September offer excellent conditions with noticeably fewer crowds. November through January is the northeast monsoon: prices are lowest but rain is frequent and ocean swimming is rough. Avoid October if possible — it is statistically the wettest month (average 400mm rainfall). For a detailed month-by-month guide, see our best time to visit Da Nang guide.
Are Da Nang hotels safe?
Da Nang is one of Vietnam's safest cities and consistently ranks among Southeast Asia's safest destinations for solo travellers, including women travelling alone. Petty crime exists as in any tourist area but is genuinely rare by regional standards. Every reputable hotel on this list uses 24-hour security, in-room safes, and key-card access. The beach areas are well-lit and patrolled at night. As of 2026, Da Nang has had no significant security incidents affecting tourism and maintains its reputation as Vietnam's most liveable city.
Where do Korean tourists stay in Da Nang?
Korean visitors — the largest foreign tourist group in Da Nang — concentrate heavily in the My Khe Beach zone, particularly the northern stretch near An Thuong village. Properties like Wyndham Soleil, TMS Hotel Da Nang Beach, and A La Carte attract significant Korean clientele. The An Thuong neighbourhood has evolved into a Korean-friendly dining and café corridor, with Korean-language signage visible in many restaurant windows. Non Nuoc Beach (near the Marble Mountains) is the second preference for Korean group tours. Hotels with Korean-speaking front desk staff include Wyndham Soleil and several mid-range properties on An Thuong Street.
How far are Da Nang hotels from the airport?
Da Nang International Airport (DAD) is centrally located — one of the most conveniently positioned airports in Southeast Asia. My Khe Beach hotels: 10–12 minutes. My An Beach hotels: 15–18 minutes. Hai Chau city centre: 15–20 minutes. Non Nuoc Beach: 20–25 minutes. Son Tra Peninsula: 20–30 minutes. Grab taxi is reliable and inexpensive: expect to pay $4–8 for most hotel zones. Pre-arranged airport transfers from hotels typically cost $8–15 for the one-way trip. See our airport transfer guide for full logistics.
