Da Nang has a way of confounding expectations. Arrive expecting a transit hub between Hoi An and Hue, and what you find instead is a city with genuine character: 30 kilometres of clean beach on the South China Sea, a compact and walkable city centre, a MICHELIN-recognised dining scene, and a hotel infrastructure that ranges from $20 guesthouses in the backstreets of Hai Chau to five-star resorts on the Son Tra Peninsula that hold their own against the best in Southeast Asia.

What makes Da Nang's hotel market unusual is the coexistence of real quality across price brackets. A $70 beach hotel here typically has a rooftop pool and staff who are genuinely helpful. A $200 resort will give you the kind of beach service that costs twice as much in Phuket. The luxury end â€” led by the InterContinental Sun Peninsula and the Sheraton Grand â€” is world-class, and consistently underpriced relative to equivalent properties in the Maldives or coastal Thailand.

This guide is built on direct local knowledge and updated seasonally. We cover the hotels that consistently deliver on their promises across beachfront, luxury, boutique, and family categories, plus a neighbourhood breakdown to help you choose the right base for your trip. Where to Stay in Da Nang

Da Nang divides across four distinct accommodation zones. Your choice shapes how your trip feels day-to-day more than any individual hotel decision. Here's what to expect from each.

My Khe Beach
Best for most visitors

The main hotel strip and Da Nang's most visited neighbourhood. My Khe Beach runs for 10 kilometres from the foot of Son Tra down to Non Nuoc, with the densest concentration of restaurants, beach bars, and hotels in the middle stretch near the An Thuong village area. Beach access is direct from most properties. The trade-off is noise and some generic resort atmosphere in peak season (Juneâ€“August). Still the right choice for first-time visitors who want everything convenient.

Son Tra Peninsula
Best for seclusion

A protected nature reserve 20 minutes from the city, Son Tra is where Da Nang hides its most dramatic accommodation. The InterContinental is the headline act, but the peninsula also has mid-range resorts that offer genuine privacy and jungle-meets-sea settings. You'll need a car or motorbike to explore â€” nothing is walkable â€” but the trade-off is real seclusion, quieter beaches, and views that justify the distance.

Han River / City Centre
Best for business & city life

The Han River strip is walkable, dense with good restaurants (including several MICHELIN-recognised spots), and close to Dragon Bridge, the Da Nang Museum, and the night market. Beach is 10â€“15 minutes by taxi from most properties. Best for business travellers, short layovers, and those using Da Nang as a base for day trips to Hoi An and Hue rather than a beach holiday proper.

Non Nuoc Beach
Best for quiet & space

At the southern end of the My Khe coastline, Non Nuoc is measurably less crowded and has some of Da Nang's best resort properties â€” the Hyatt Regency, Platinum Retreat, and several large Vietnamese resort brands. Marble Mountains are within walking distance, and Hoi An is under 40 minutes south. The downside: you're a 20-minute taxi ride from the city's restaurant scene, which matters on a short trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What area is best to stay in Da Nang?
My Khe Beach is the best all-round base for most visitors. You're on the sand, within walking distance of the city's best independent restaurants, and 20 minutes from Hoi An. Son Tra Peninsula is the right choice if you prioritise seclusion and resort atmosphere over urban convenience. The Han River strip suits business travellers and those who prefer a city base to a beach one.
Is it better to stay near My Khe Beach?
For most leisure travellers, yes. My Khe gives you direct beach access, strong restaurant and cafÃ© options in the adjacent An Thuong village, and easy taxi or Grab access to the rest of the city. Hotels here span every price bracket from $30 guesthouses to $400-a-night beachfront villas. The trade-off is that the strip can feel crowded and a little generic in peak season (Juneâ€“August). If you prefer quieter surroundings, Non Nuoc Beach 15 minutes south has comparable hotel quality with notably fewer crowds.
Are there luxury resorts in Da Nang?
Yes â€” and more than most people expect. The InterContinental Sun Peninsula Resort on Son Tra Peninsula has consistently placed in CondÃ© Nast Traveler's global top-hotel rankings and is genuinely world-class. Other strong luxury options include the Sheraton Grand Danang, the Hyatt Regency Non Nuoc, the Pullman, and the Four Points by Sheraton. Rates at the luxury end typically run $180â€“$400 per night, which positions Da Nang as significantly better value than comparable resort quality in the Maldives, coastal Thailand, or Bali.
How many days should you spend in Da Nang?
Three to five days is the right range for a standalone Da Nang trip. Three days covers the essentials: beach time, a day at Ba Na Hills or Marble Mountains, and an afternoon or evening in Hoi An (30 minutes south). Five days allows you to slow down â€” a day at Non Nuoc, time to explore the Son Tra Peninsula, a food-focused evening in the city. Many visitors build Da Nang into a broader Central Vietnam itinerary that includes Hoi An and Hue, typically spanning 7â€“10 days total.
