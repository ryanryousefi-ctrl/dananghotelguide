Da Nang's boutique hotel scene â€” an honest picture

The honest comparison point for Da Nang's boutique scene is Hoi An, 30 minutes south, which has been a boutique hotel market for over two decades and has the depth to show it: dozens of distinctive small properties spanning converted merchant houses, garden villas, and genuinely characterful guesthouses with long-running owners who have shaped their hotels around the town they know. Da Nang is not at that level. The boutique market here is newer, smaller, and somewhat concentrated in a single neighbourhood â€” but the properties that have emerged from it are genuinely good.

The An Thuong village area â€” a compact grid of streets running behind the northern section of My Khe Beach â€” is where Da Nang's boutique scene has taken root. The neighbourhood has the right conditions: a local residential character, a density of independent cafÃ©s and restaurants that gives it a lived-in quality, and enough footfall from beachgoers to sustain small hospitality businesses without becoming over-touristed. Several Vietnamese-owned boutique properties have opened here in the past four years, and a few international-design-influenced guesthouses have followed.

What you get from Da Nang's boutique properties is broadly consistent: better breakfast than chain hotels at the same price, staff who give recommendations drawn from local knowledge rather than Tripadvisor, rooms that have been designed rather than specified from a brand manual, and the experience of staying in a neighbourhood rather than on a resort compound. What you don't get: multiple pools, beach clubs, spa suites, or the anonymous efficiency of an international hotel.
