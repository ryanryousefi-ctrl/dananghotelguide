Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang Review

From $155/night ★ 8.5 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

Vinpearl is Vietnam's largest domestic hotel and resort brand, operated by Vingroup — one of the country's biggest conglomerates. The Da Nang property on Non Nuoc Beach is one of the brand's flagship city resorts, and follows the Vinpearl model of large-scale, fully self-contained resort infrastructure aimed primarily at Vietnamese domestic travellers and regional Asian tourists.

Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang operates at a five-star level and delivers consistent quality across room standards, pool facilities, and dining — driven by the brand's substantial investment in quality control across its properties. For travellers familiar with other Vinpearl properties, the Da Nang resort will feel reassuringly predictable. For those new to the brand, it is a strong introduction to what Vietnamese domestic luxury hospitality has achieved.

Location

Non Nuoc Beach is quieter than the My Khe strip further north. At 12 km from Da Nang city centre, the resort is genuinely south-of-the-city in feel, with the Marble Mountains nearby and Hoi An accessible in around 25 minutes by car. The location suits guests who want a resort holiday rather than a city break.

  • Airport: 14 km / 20 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 25 min taxi
  • Marble Mountains: 5 min taxi
  • Hoi An: 25 km / 30 min by car
  • Non Nuoc Beach: direct access

Rooms and Design

The 200-plus rooms and suites are spacious by Vietnamese domestic hotel standards — entry-level rooms at around 50 square metres with balconies and either pool or ocean views. Design is polished contemporary with Vietnamese decorative elements. Bathrooms are large and well-appointed. The resort's size means a range of room categories is available, from garden-view rooms to premium beachfront suites.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

Multiple outdoor pools including a main lagoon pool and children's pool. The beach is a long, well-maintained stretch of Non Nuoc sand with full sunlounger and umbrella service. A full spa, fitness centre, kids' club, and an array of watersports are available. Vinpearl's kids' club is among the better-run in Da Nang, which contributes to the resort's family-friendly reputation.

Dining Options

Multiple restaurants serve Vietnamese, Asian, and international cuisine. The main buffet restaurant is a particularly strong offering — a wide spread encompassing Vietnamese regional dishes, Japanese-influenced options, and international staples. A beach bar and pool bar operate through the day.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): $155–$210/night
  • Shoulder season: $210–$300/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $280–$420/night

Who This Hotel Is Best For

Families visiting Da Nang who want a complete resort package, domestic Vietnamese travellers seeking familiar quality benchmarks, and guests planning to combine the resort with day trips to Hoi An and the Marble Mountains. It is a strong choice for first-time visitors to Da Nang who want reliable five-star quality without the premium of the InterContinental or Melia.

Pros
  • Large-scale resort with comprehensive family facilities
  • Strong kids' club and children's pool facilities
  • Quality-consistent Vinpearl brand standards
  • Good buffet spread covering Vietnamese and international cuisine
  • Direct Non Nuoc Beach access on a quieter stretch of coast
  • Proximity to Marble Mountains for cultural excursions
Cons
  • 12 km from city centre — taxis required for most excursions
  • Resort scale can feel impersonal compared to boutique properties
  • Design lacks the distinctive character of top competitors
  • Booking.com reviews occasionally cite inconsistency in service

Final Verdict

Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang is a strong family resort with all the facilities required for a comfortable, self-contained beach holiday. The Vinpearl brand delivers consistent quality, and the Non Nuoc Beach location is genuinely pleasant. For travellers who want something more architecturally distinctive, the nearby Melia or Hyatt Regency may be more appealing. But for reliable five-star comfort at competitive rates, Vinpearl's track record is hard to argue with.

Is Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang Worth It?

Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang occupies one of the most dramatic positions of any hotel in the city — the northern tip of Son Tra Peninsula, with sea views on multiple sides. As a Vietnamese luxury brand competing with the IHG, Marriott, and Hyatt properties, it delivers a genuine luxury experience with some local character. Rates are often lower than comparable international brands, making it appealing for those curious about premium Vietnamese hospitality. The honest caveat is that service and F&B consistency is generally below the InterContinental or Hyatt Regency. For travellers who want a spectacular location, lower rates than the InterContinental, and don't require impeccable five-star service standards, Vinpearl Luxury is a worthwhile and sometimes excellent choice.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From Vinpearl Luxury Da Nang, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • Son Tra Peninsula — On-site. Forested headland with wildlife, hiking trails, and Lady Buddha viewpoint.
  • My Khe Beach — 15 min taxi south. The main beach strip — quick access for a day on the sand.
  • Dragon Bridge — 20 min taxi. Da Nang's most photographed landmark.
  • Han River — 20 min taxi. City-centre riverside dining and entertainment.
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — 40 min by car. Must-do day trip for visitors to the Da Nang region.
  • Lady Buddha Statue (Linh Ung Pagoda) — 10 min taxi. The 67-metre white Buddha statue with panoramic Da Nang Bay views.
