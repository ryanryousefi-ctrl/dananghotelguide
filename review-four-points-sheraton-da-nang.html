Four Points by Sheraton Danang Review

From $80/night ★ 8.4 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

Four Points by Sheraton Danang is part of Marriott's accessible upper-midscale brand, positioned as a more affordable entry point into the Bonvoy loyalty ecosystem than the nearby Sheraton Grand. It sits directly on My Khe Beach and offers the key requirements of a Da Nang beach hotel — beach access, a pool, reliable Wi-Fi, and consistent room standards — at rates notably below the five-star properties that dominate the strip.

The hotel functions well as an honest, no-surprises four-star beach hotel. It does not attempt to compete on resort atmosphere or architectural distinction, and it doesn't need to. For Bonvoy members accumulating or spending points, it represents one of the better value redemption options in Da Nang. For non-members, the beach location and competitive rates still make it a compelling choice.

Location

The My Khe Beach location is directly on the main coastal strip, well-positioned for both beach access and city exploration. Dragon Bridge is 12 minutes by taxi. The airport is 7 km away. A wide range of local seafood restaurants and Vietnamese street food spots are walkable — a genuine advantage for travellers who want to eat well without committing to on-site restaurant prices every night.

  • Airport: 7 km / 12 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 12 min taxi
  • Han Market: 18 min taxi
  • Hoi An: 30 min by car
  • Local restaurants: 5 min walk

Rooms and Design

Rooms run around 30–40 square metres — compact but well-planned with the Four Points design standard of clean, unfussy contemporary styling. Ocean-facing rooms on higher floors are the best category and provide genuine My Khe Beach views. Bathrooms are functional and well-maintained. The beds — a Four Points brand strength — are consistently praised for comfort in guest reviews.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

An outdoor pool is positioned between the hotel building and the beach. It is a modest size relative to five-star resort pools but clean and functional. Direct beach access is the hotel's strongest amenity. A fitness centre and basic spa round out the offer.

Dining Options

The main restaurant operates a standard hotel buffet for breakfast and all-day dining. A pool bar serves drinks and snacks. Given the walkability of the local My Khe restaurant strip, most guests sensibly explore beyond the hotel for dinner.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): $80–$115/night
  • Shoulder season: $115–$165/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $150–$230/night

Who This Hotel Is Best For

Marriott Bonvoy members, budget-conscious travellers who don't want to sacrifice beach access, and those who prioritise location and value over resort facilities. Also works well as a base for short trips combining Da Nang city exploration with My Khe Beach time.

Pros
  • Direct My Khe Beach access at below-five-star rates
  • Marriott Bonvoy points earned and redeemable
  • Walking distance to local restaurant strip
  • 7 km from airport — fast transfer
  • Reliable and consistent four-star standards
  • Good value for ocean-view rooms on upper floors
Cons
  • Smaller rooms than resort competitors
  • Pool modest compared to five-star rivals
  • Design lacks the character of boutique alternatives
  • On-site dining limited — reliance on nearby restaurants

Final Verdict

Four Points by Sheraton Danang delivers exactly what it promises: reliable four-star quality on My Khe Beach at competitive rates. For Marriott Bonvoy members it is a smart points redemption. For non-members it offers genuine beach access at prices that make the five-star options on either side of it seem excessive if all you need is a clean room and a path to the sand.

Is the Four Points by Sheraton Danang Worth It?

For value-focused travellers who want a Marriott brand on My Khe Beach, yes. The Four Points by Sheraton Danang punches above its midscale positioning in several areas — the beach access is direct, the pool is well-maintained, and the room quality is consistent with Sheraton standards at a lower price. For Marriott Bonvoy members who want points-earning at a beach hotel without paying Sheraton Grand or Marriott Resort rates, this is a logical choice. The main honest caveat: the restaurant and bar offerings are not a reason to stay in, and the immediate beach strip can feel busy and slightly commercialised compared to quieter southern stretches. But at $90–$140/night in low season, it delivers real My Khe Beach access at a price few competing properties can match.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From Four Points by Sheraton Danang, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • My Khe Beach — On-site. Direct beachfront — one of Da Nang's most accessible stretches of sand.
  • Dragon Bridge — 10 min taxi. Easy taxi hop to Da Nang's most photographed landmark.
  • My Khe Restaurant Strip — 2 min walk. Dozens of seafood restaurants and street food stalls directly nearby.
  • Han River — 12 min taxi. Riverside city area with markets, restaurants, and the Love Bridge.
  • Da Nang Airport — 8 km / 12 min taxi. One of the quickest airport-to-beach connections in Da Nang.
  • Marble Mountains — 20 min taxi south. Five marble hills — a natural and cultural landmark worth visiting.
