Why Da Nang beats Phuket and Bali

Da Nang occupies a rare sweet spot: five-star resorts on a world-class coastline at prices that undercut comparable destinations by 30–50%. My Khe Beach — one of Asia's most consistently rated urban beaches — runs 30 kilometres of white sand directly accessible from hotels at every price point. A beachfront four-star with a rooftop infinity pool can still be secured for under $100 outside peak season.

The accommodation landscape divides across three corridors. The My Khe strip is the most popular base — walkable to the beach, Da Nang's best restaurants, and the lively An Thuong village bar scene. Browse all Da Nang hotels to filter by category and live Booking.com rates. The Son Tra Peninsula offers the most dramatic resort experiences — private clifftop infinity pools, lush jungle settings, and near-total seclusion. The Han River strip suits business travellers and city explorers who want to be minutes from Dragon Bridge, the Marble Mountains, and Hoi An, 30 minutes south.

Best travel window: March through August when beach conditions are perfect — calm South China Sea, sustained sunshine, and sea temperatures around 28°C. November through February offers 30–40% discounts on peak rates, though northeast monsoon swells make ocean swimming rougher. Read the full best time to visit Da Nang guide and month-by-month weather breakdown.

Beyond accommodation, the MICHELIN-recognised dining scene spans 43 restaurants — one MICHELIN Star, one Green Star, 20 Bib Gourmand and 22 Selected listings — anchored by street food centred on mì Quảng, bánh mì, and grilled seafood fresh from the morning catch. Every price bracket is covered within walking distance of My Khe Beach.
