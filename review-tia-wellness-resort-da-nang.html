TIA Wellness Resort Review

From $250/night ★ 9.0 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

TIA Wellness Resort occupies a small, focused beachfront plot on My Khe Beach and operates on a model completely unlike any other hotel in Da Nang. All stays are all-inclusive: meals, unlimited spa treatments, fitness classes, and wellness consultations are bundled into the nightly rate. The resort has just 58 rooms and suites, which allows for a personalised, retreat-like experience rather than the anonymous scale of larger resorts.

TIA was built specifically for wellness-focused travellers — those who come to Da Nang for a detox, yoga programme, or spa retreat rather than conventional beach tourism. The concept is unusual in Southeast Asia and particularly rare in Vietnam, where wellness facilities typically exist as hotel amenities rather than the entire point of a property. The result is a genuinely differentiated product.

Location

Despite its wellness focus, TIA has a very convenient location on My Khe Beach — 5 km from Dragon Bridge and 7 km from the airport. The city is easily accessible for guests who want to balance wellness programming with local food exploration. Unlike the more isolated resort properties on Son Tra or Non Nuoc, TIA's position on the main beach strip means urban convenience is available when desired.

  • Airport: 7 km / 12 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 12 min taxi
  • Han Market: 15 min taxi
  • Hoi An: 30 min by car
  • My Khe Beach: direct access

Rooms and Design

The 58 rooms and suites are designed with wellness in mind — natural materials, low-EMF considerations in some room categories, organic bedding, air purification, and yoga mats as standard. Rooms are sizeable at 45–80 square metres, with many offering garden or ocean views. The design is calm and deliberately uncluttered, supporting the detox-and-restore concept.

The Presidential Suite and beachfront suites are the premium options, with private pools and expanded spa facilities. For a wellness retreat, the investment in one of these categories can transform the stay into something close to a private villa experience.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

An outdoor pool and hydrotherapy facilities are central to the amenities. The spa itself — spanning multiple treatment rooms, hydrotherapy pools, steam rooms, and dedicated programme spaces — is the resort's primary facility. Unlimited spa treatments are included in all rates, with a full menu of massages, body treatments, facials, and therapies drawn from traditional Vietnamese and Asian wellness practices.

Daily yoga classes, meditation sessions, fitness programmes, nutritional consultations, and cooking classes are part of the included offering. Beach access is direct on My Khe Beach.

Dining Options

All meals are included in TIA's rates. The food philosophy centres on nutritious, flavour-forward Vietnamese and international cuisine — designed by the resort's nutrition team rather than a conventional hotel F&B department. Organic ingredients, functional foods, and reduced-sugar menus feature prominently. Despite the health focus, guest reviews consistently praise the food quality as genuinely satisfying rather than punishingly restrictive.

Typical Price Range

TIA's all-inclusive rates make direct comparisons to room-only hotels difficult. The headline rate covers accommodation, all meals, unlimited spa treatments, and all wellness programming.

  • Low season (May–August): $250–$350/night (all-inclusive)
  • Shoulder season: $330–$430/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $400–$560/night
  • Multi-night retreat packages available with additional discounts

Who This Hotel Is Best For

TIA is specifically designed for travellers seeking a wellness retreat — detox programmes, burnout recovery, post-operation recuperation, or structured health holidays. It appeals strongly to solo female travellers, couples seeking a digital detox, and professionals on restorative breaks. It is not suited to travellers who want a conventional beach holiday, a lively pool scene, or standard resort entertainment.

Pros
  • Unique all-inclusive wellness concept unavailable elsewhere in Da Nang
  • Unlimited spa treatments genuinely included — exceptional value
  • Only 58 rooms — intimate and personalised service
  • Thoughtful nutritious dining that doesn't compromise on taste
  • Daily yoga, meditation and fitness programming included
  • My Khe Beach location more convenient than isolated wellness retreats
Cons
  • Higher nightly rates than conventional hotels
  • Not suitable for travellers seeking a conventional beach resort
  • Limited entertainment — no nightlife, limited bar scene
  • Small property — no large pool or resort-scale facilities
  • Strict wellness ethos may feel restrictive for some

Final Verdict

TIA Wellness Resort is the most distinctive hotel product in Da Nang — a genuine wellness retreat that delivers on its concept at a meaningful level. If the all-inclusive wellness model fits the purpose of your trip, the value for the nightly rate is exceptional: unlimited spa treatments alone at any comparable Da Nang hotel would cost far more. For the right traveller with the right intention, TIA is an easy recommendation.

Is TIA Wellness Resort Da Nang Worth It?

For travellers whose primary goal is wellness — structured programmes, daily yoga, detox retreats, and a retreat from digital life — TIA Wellness Resort is one of the best options not just in Da Nang, but in the wider region. It is not competing with the same guests as the Hyatt Regency or Hilton. The pool villa experience, included wellness activities, and the quality of the spa and healthy dining make the premium rate defensible for its target audience. Where it falls short is as a conventional beach hotel: it is designed for immersive stays of four nights or more, and guests who book for a single night seeking a beach resort will not get the full value of what TIA offers. Book it for the wellness programmes. Stay for at least four nights.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From TIA Wellness Resort Da Nang, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • Non-Nuoc Beach — On-site. Direct beach access on Da Nang's quietest and most scenic coastal stretch.
  • Marble Mountains — 5 min taxi. The resort's closest cultural attraction — five limestone hills with temples.
  • Furama Resort & Naman Retreat — 5 min walk. Neighbouring properties — useful context for comparison.
  • My Khe Beach strip — 15 min taxi. The main beach restaurant and nightlife corridor.
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — 25 min by car. The must-do day trip for any Da Nang visitor.
  • Dragon Bridge — 20 min taxi. Worth the trip on a Friday or Saturday night for the fire show.
