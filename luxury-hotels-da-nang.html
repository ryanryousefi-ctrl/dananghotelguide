Overview

Why Da Nang for Luxury Travel

Da Nang's luxury hotels span three distinct settings: clifftop jungle, open beachfront, and city river views

The case for Da Nang as a luxury destination is ultimately a value argument — and it's a compelling one. A five-star resort here that commands $180 a night would cost $350–$500 in the Maldives, Bali, or coastal Thailand. The InterContinental Sun Peninsula, which holds a consistent position on Condé Nast Traveler's global top-hotel lists, costs less per night than a mid-range beach bungalow in Bora Bora.

What you get in exchange for the price differential is genuinely world-class: butler service, spa programmes that rival the best in Southeast Asia, room quality that holds up under scrutiny, and food and beverage operations anchored by Da Nang's only MICHELIN-starred restaurant. The luxury hotels here are not compromise options. They are destinations in their own right.

This guide covers all five credible five-star properties in Da Nang — the ones that deliver on their category promises — with honest assessments of who each one suits. See also the complete Da Nang hotel guide for options across all budgets.

✦ The value argument

Da Nang luxury rates are 40–60% lower than comparable resorts in the Maldives, Bali, or coastal Thailand. The InterContinental Sun Peninsula — ranked in Condé Nast Traveler's Top 10 Resort Hotels in Asia — starts from $340/night. An equivalent property in the Maldives starts from $800.

Editor's Choice

InterContinental Sun Peninsula Resort

The resort's cascading architecture follows the jungle hillside, ensuring every room opens onto canopy or sea views

The most-awarded property in Central Vietnam and — on most metrics — the finest resort in the country. Set within the protected Son Tra Nature Reserve on a forested hillside above a private bay, the InterContinental was designed by architect Bill Bensley with a specific brief: build something spectacular without destroying the environment it sits in. The result is a cascading series of structures that follow the hillside contour, with rooms and villas that open onto jungle canopy rather than car parks.

La Maison 1888, the on-site restaurant, holds Da Nang's only MICHELIN Star and is helmed by a French executive chef working with Central Vietnamese ingredients. The dining experience here is worth the 20-minute transfer from the city regardless of where you're staying. Transfers from Da Nang city are included in the room rate.

The private bay below the resort has calm water sheltered from the South China Sea swell, making it genuinely swimmable year-round — a significant advantage during the October–November monsoon period when My Khe Beach is effectively closed.

Best for Families & Space

Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort & Spa

Hyatt Regency Da Nang — six hectares on Non Nuoc Beach, pools terracing down to privately maintained sand

Five-Star
Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort & Spa
📍 Non Nuoc Beach · 6-hectare compound · 15 km south of city

Six hectares, four terraced pools descending to the sand, a serious kids' programme, and room categories from garden standards to private pool villas. FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the InterContinental Sun Peninsula worth the price?
For the right trip, yes. The InterContinental is genuinely world-class — it holds a place on Condé Nast Traveler's global top-hotel lists — and the on-site MICHELIN Star restaurant (La Maison 1888) makes it a destination in its own right. At $340+ per night it's not cheap in absolute terms, but relative to what $340 buys you in the Maldives or Bali, it represents exceptional value. Best for honeymooners, milestone trips, and anyone who wants to spend several nights without needing to leave the property.
What's the best luxury hotel on the beach in Da Nang?
The Sheraton Grand Danang Resort on My Khe Beach is the best combination of five-star quality and direct beach access. Three pools, a proper beach club, pool villas with direct sand gates, and the An Thuong restaurant scene within walking distance. The Pullman Da Nang Beach Resort offers comparable quality at a lower price point and is worth considering if Sheraton rates are elevated on your dates.
Which luxury hotel is best for families in Da Nang?
The Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort & Spa at Non Nuoc Beach is the best luxury option for families. Six hectares of resort, four terraced pools including dedicated children's areas, a serious kids' programme, and room categories with properly configured family layouts. The beach at Non Nuoc is calmer than My Khe, which matters significantly for families with young children. See the full family hotel guide for more detail.
Is there a luxury hotel in Da Nang city centre?
The Four Points by Sheraton on the Han River is the only serious city-centre luxury option. Upper-floor rooms have Dragon Bridge views, service is strong, and the rooftop pool is one of the better cityscape vantage points in Da Nang. Beach access requires a 10–15 minute Grab ride, which is fine for business-focused stays but less convenient for beach-holiday itineraries.
