Novotel Danang Premier Han River Review

From $75/night ★ 8.3 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

The Novotel Danang Premier Han River is a 39-floor tower on the west bank of the Han River, directly opposite Dragon Bridge. It is one of the most centrally located hotels in Da Nang and appeals primarily to travellers who want a city base rather than a beach resort — those who plan to explore Da Nang's food markets, cultural sites, and surrounding attractions, and treat beach time as an excursion rather than the default activity.

As a Novotel property it operates under Accor's midscale-upscale banner, which means reliable service standards, loyalty points through the ALL programme, and infrastructure built for both leisure and business travellers. Rates are consistently competitive for what is delivered.

Location

The Han River location is genuinely excellent for city exploration. Dragon Bridge — which breathes fire on weekend evenings — is visible from the hotel and a short walk away. Han Market, a hub of local commerce and street food, is 10 minutes on foot. My Khe Beach is a five-kilometre taxi ride. The airport is just 3 km away, making this one of the most convenient arrival-to-hotel journeys in the city.

The city-centre position means the hotel is surrounded by Da Nang's best restaurant streets, coffee shops, and evening nightlife. For those who view food exploration as a core part of travel, the Han River area is one of the best-placed hotel options in Da Nang.

  • Airport: 3 km / 8 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 5 min walk
  • Han Market: 10 min walk
  • My Khe Beach: 10 min taxi
  • Hoi An: 30 min by car

Rooms and Design

The 323 rooms occupy floors across the tower, with the higher floors offering impressive river, city, and distant sea views. Rooms are in the 30–40 square metre range — compact for a four-star hotel but efficiently designed. The signature views from upper floors are a genuine USP, especially rooms directly facing Dragon Bridge.

Design is contemporary and clean without being particularly distinctive. Bathrooms are functional with decent finishing. The Premier Floor rooms add access to a business lounge with complimentary breakfast and evening canapes.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

The rooftop pool on an upper floor offers one of the best pool views in Da Nang — city skyline, the Han River, and on clear days a glimpse of the sea. It is a small to mid-sized pool rather than a sprawling resort complex, but the view compensates. A fitness centre and spa are available on the premises.

Dining Options

The Square Restaurant operates the main breakfast buffet and all-day dining. A rooftop bar and a lobby-level cafe complete the on-site options. The hotel is a five-minute walk from Da Nang's best independent restaurants and street food stalls, which is where most guests sensibly end up for dinner.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): $75–$105/night
  • Shoulder season: $100–$145/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $130–$200/night

Who This Hotel Is Best For

Travellers who prioritise exploring Da Nang's city life over beach relaxation, business visitors, short-stay transit guests, and Accor ALL loyalty members will get the most out of the Novotel Premier. It is also a strong choice for those travelling to both Da Nang and Hoi An on the same trip, given its central location and easy onward connection.

Pros
  • Excellent city-centre location opposite Dragon Bridge
  • 3 km from the airport — fastest transfer among major hotels
  • Walking distance to Da Nang's best restaurants and markets
  • Rooftop pool with city and river views
  • Accor ALL loyalty points earned
  • Competitive rates for a 4-star hotel
Cons
  • No beach access — 5 km from My Khe
  • Rooms smaller than resort alternatives
  • Pool is compact compared to beachfront resorts
  • Street noise on lower floors facing the river road

Final Verdict

The Novotel Danang Premier Han River is the standout choice for city-focused travellers — those who want to eat well, explore Da Nang on foot, and be close to cultural landmarks. It is not a beach resort and makes no attempt to be one. At its price point, it delivers excellent value for a centrally positioned hotel with strong city views and a walkable location. The 3 km airport distance is a bonus that frequent travellers will appreciate.

Our verdict
8.1 / 10

The best city-centre option for travellers who want Da Nang's restaurant scene, Dragon Bridge views, and easy airport access without paying resort rates.
