What Does a Hotel in Da Nang Actually Cost?

Da Nang hotel prices don't follow a simple high-season, low-season split. They're shaped by at least four overlapping forces: the beach season (May‚ÄìAugust, when Vietnamese domestic tourism peaks), the rainy season (September‚ÄìNovember, which deflates demand sharply), the Tet holiday period (typically late January to mid-February, which spikes prices across all categories), and growing international inbound ‚Äî particularly from South Korea, which now accounts for a significant share of Da Nang's foreign visitor arrivals.

The price index on this page was compiled by manually sampling rates across 20 representative hotels in Da Nang's three main market tiers ‚Äî 3-star city hotels, 4-star beach hotels, and 5-star resort properties. Rates were cross-referenced against booking platforms and property rate sheets from late 2025 and early 2026. This is not an automated aggregation; it is a curated editorial index intended to give travelers a realistic planning baseline. For a full breakdown of what a trip costs beyond accommodation, see our Da Nang Travel Cost Guide.

What These Numbers Mean in Practice

The savings potential in Da Nang is unusually steep compared to other Southeast Asian beach destinations. A 5-star resort that averages $260/night in peak summer can legitimately be booked for $100‚Äì$130 in October ‚Äî that's the same property, often with the same amenities and service levels, for less than half the price. The compression is driven by the hard stop of rainy season: most leisure travelers won't book Da Nang in October regardless of price, which forces properties to discount aggressively.

For budget travelers, the 3-star market in low season is exceptionally competitive ‚Äî $22‚Äì$28/night for clean, well-located city properties puts Da Nang in the same affordability bracket as budget travelers' favorites like Chiang Mai or Canggu.

What's Driving the Change?

Korean inbound tourism has been the most significant demand-side factor reshaping Da Nang's hotel market over the past two years. Direct routes from Seoul, Busan, and Jeju have expanded substantially, and Korean travelers ‚Äî who typically favor 4-star and above properties ‚Äî have added a second demand peak in April and November that didn't exist with the same intensity in 2022‚Äì2023. This has structurally lifted 4-star pricing year-round.

International recovery from pandemic-era lows continued through 2024 and 2025, with European and Australian visitor numbers returning to near pre-2020 levels. This broad-based recovery supported price increases across all categories without the capacity constraints that might otherwise check growth.

Inflation in operating costs ‚Äî particularly energy, labor, and food and beverage ‚Äî has pushed minimum viable rates upward for most properties. The ~16‚Äì19% increase seen in 3-star and 4-star categories over three years tracks closely with Vietnam's cumulative hospitality sector cost inflation over the same period.

Increased 5-star supply has partially offset pricing pressure at the top end. Several new luxury properties opened along Non Nuoc Beach between 2023 and 2025, expanding available inventory and moderating rate growth. The 5-star segment's 8% three-year increase is the most restrained of the three tiers as a result. This dynamic is likely to continue through 2026 as additional pipeline properties come online.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Da Nang expensive compared to Phuket or Bali?

Da Nang is meaningfully cheaper than both Phuket and Bali across all hotel categories. A comparable 4-star beachfront property that costs $150‚Äì$200/night in Phuket or $130‚Äì$180 in Bali's Seminyak typically runs $70‚Äì$110 in Da Nang during peak season. Five-star resorts tell a similar story: Da Nang's luxury tier averages $200‚Äì$350 at peak, while equivalent Phuket properties regularly exceed $400‚Äì$600. The gap narrows slightly during Da Nang's low season, but it remains the most affordable of the three at nearly every market tier. Food and transport costs are also lower, meaning the overall trip cost difference is even greater than the hotel gap alone suggests.

When are hotels cheapest in Da Nang?

October and November represent Da Nang's true low season and offer the cheapest hotel prices of the year. During these months, 3-star hotels average $22‚Äì$28 per night, 4-star properties drop to $45‚Äì$58, and even 5-star resorts can be found for $90‚Äì$130 ‚Äî roughly 55‚Äì60% below their peak rates. The trade-off is weather: October and November sit in Da Nang's rainy season, with the highest rainfall typically concentrated in mid-October through mid-November. September is also low season but slightly wetter on average. If avoiding rain is a priority, late November or early December offers rising prices but markedly improved conditions and thinner crowds than the summer peak.

How far in advance should I book Da Nang hotels?

The right booking window varies sharply by season. For Tet (late January/February) and peak summer (June‚ÄìAugust), book 6‚Äì10 weeks in advance ‚Äî quality properties at mid-range prices sell out quickly and rates spike with short booking windows. For shoulder months like March, April, and December, 3‚Äì4 weeks is generally sufficient with good room selection still available. During low season (September‚ÄìNovember), last-minute booking is viable and sometimes advantageous, as properties discount aggressively to fill occupancy. For 5-star resorts during any peak period, early booking is strongly recommended: room types, pool villa categories, and promotional packages have limited availability and are rarely discounted close to arrival.

Are 5-star resorts worth it in Da Nang?

For the value they deliver relative to global luxury hotel markets, Da Nang's 5-star resorts are among the strongest value propositions in Southeast Asia. Properties along My Khe Beach and Non Nuoc ‚Äî including internationally branded resorts ‚Äî offer private beach access, multiple dining outlets, full spa facilities, and generous room sizes at prices that would buy a mid-range city hotel in most Western capitals. The clearest value window is shoulder season: late March, April, or December, when rates sit 35‚Äì45% below peak but facilities are fully operational and weather is at its most favorable. Even peak season rates of $200‚Äì$350 are reasonable given the level of service, setting, and inclusions that most Da Nang resorts bundle into their packages.
