Silk Path Grand Resort & Spa Danang Review

Last reviewed: March 2026

From $140/night ★ 8.7 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

Silk Path Grand Resort & Spa Danang is operated by Silk Path Hotels, a growing Vietnamese luxury hotel group with properties in Hanoi, Hue, Sapa, and Da Nang. The Da Nang property on My Khe Beach is the group's most resort-focused property and has developed a strong reputation for its spa facilities and five-star service culture — built on Vietnamese hospitality traditions rather than a generic international chain model.

The property is relatively new compared to the established international brands on the same strip, having opened in the late 2010s. It has benefited from this timing by incorporating contemporary design and facilities standards from inception rather than retrofitting an older build.

Location

My Khe Beach, 6 km from Dragon Bridge and 8 km from the airport. Local restaurants are walkable. Hoi An is 30 minutes south.

  • Airport: 8 km / 12 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 14 min taxi
  • Local restaurants: 5 min walk
  • Hoi An: 30 min by car
  • My Khe Beach: direct access

Rooms and Design

The 324 rooms run from around 40 square metres for entry-level Superior rooms to generous suites. The design integrates Vietnamese silk motifs and craft elements into a modern luxury framework — more distinctive than generic international chain interiors, though not as dramatically individual as Naman Retreat or the InterContinental. Ocean-view balcony rooms are the most popular category.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

A resort pool complex with adult and family sections sits adjacent to the beach. Direct My Khe Beach access is well-managed. The spa is the hotel's defining amenity — a substantial facility with a full Vietnamese and Asian treatment menu, thermal experiences, and wellness programming. A fitness centre, tennis courts, and kids' club complete the offer.

Dining Options

Multiple restaurants serve Vietnamese, Chinese, Japanese, and international cuisine. The main buffet restaurant is well-regarded for breakfast quality. A rooftop bar and pool bar are available. The breadth of cuisine options is a strength relative to some single-concept resort restaurants.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): $140–$190/night
  • Shoulder season: $190–$275/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $260–$400/night

Who This Hotel Is Best For

Spa-focused travellers who want extensive wellness facilities alongside beach access, couples seeking a polished Vietnamese luxury experience, and guests who want the full five-star resort package at rates somewhat lower than the major international brands.

Pros
  • Extensive spa — one of the strongest spa facilities on My Khe
  • Vietnamese luxury brand delivering distinctive local character
  • Direct beach access with full service
  • Multiple cuisine options across on-site restaurants
  • Competitive rates relative to international brand competitors
Cons
  • Less brand recognition than Sheraton/Hilton/Marriott for loyalty travellers
  • Design less dramatic than Da Nang's most distinctive properties
  • Pool complex adequate but not exceptional
  • Service consistency can vary in off-peak months

Final Verdict

Silk Path Grand Resort & Spa Danang is a strong performer in the five-star My Khe Beach segment — particularly for spa-focused guests who want the most comprehensive wellness facilities in the area. The Vietnamese luxury brand ethos gives it a character that distinguishes it from the international chains alongside it, and the competitive rates make it a compelling alternative to the Sheraton Grand or Radisson Blu for guests whose loyalty isn't locked to a specific programme.

Is the Silk Path Grand Danang Worth It?

The Silk Path Grand Danang is a strong Vietnamese luxury brand hotel in the city centre that delivers competitive quality at rates below international chains of comparable standard. The Han River views from upper floors are impressive, the rooftop pool is a genuine asset, and the overall design has a polished Vietnamese character that distinguishes it from generic international hotels. For travellers who want a luxury city-centre experience, Dragon Bridge proximity, and support a Vietnamese hospitality brand, it is worth serious consideration. The main gap compared to the Grand Mercure or Novotel is in business infrastructure and loyalty programme integration. But for leisure travellers prioritising value and location, the Silk Path Grand competes strongly.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From Silk Path Grand Danang, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • Dragon Bridge — 5 min walk. Da Nang's most famous landmark is within easy walking distance.
  • Han River Waterfront — On-site. Riverside promenade, restaurants, and evening atmosphere.
  • Han Market — 10 min walk. Da Nang's main market for produce, textiles, and street food.
  • Da Nang Cathedral — 10 min walk. The pink cathedral — a central city landmark.
  • My Khe Beach — 10 min taxi. The main beach strip is a short taxi ride from the city centre.
  • Hoi An — 30 min by car. Straightforward day trip from this central Da Nang location.
