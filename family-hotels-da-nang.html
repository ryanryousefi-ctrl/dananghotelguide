Best family hotels in Da Nang

Da Nang's resort strip on My Khe and Non Nuoc beaches is well set up for families. The best properties combine supervised kids clubs, dedicated children's pools, and beach access within easy reach of Marble Mountains, Hoi An, and Ba Na Hills. Prices below are indicative mid-season rates; always check live availability.

Furama Resort Da Nang

#1 Family Pick Best for Families

Furama Resort Da Nang

★★★★★
📍 My Khe Beach, Da Nang
Kids Club 3 Pools Beachfront Water Slides Free Breakfast

The original luxury resort on My Khe Beach, Furama remains one of Da Nang's top family choices. Its Balinese-inspired grounds spread across 4 hectares with three interconnected pools including a dedicated children's pool with water slides. The Furama Kids' Club runs supervised activities for 4–12 year olds daily. Villas and family suites offer generous space, and the beachfront location means direct access to one of central Vietnam's finest stretches of sand.

Best for: families wanting a self-contained resort with excellent pool infrastructure and beach access.

Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort & Spa

#2 Family Pick 5-Star Luxury

Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort & Spa

★★★★★
📍 Non Nuoc Beach, Da Nang
Camp Hyatt 5 Pools Waterslide Beachfront Family Suites

The Hyatt Regency is purpose-built for families at scale. Its 5-pool complex includes a dedicated waterslide pool, a shallow splash zone for toddlers, and a lazy river — rare in Da Nang. Camp Hyatt runs structured programmes for children aged 3–12. The 198 rooms and residences include spacious family suites with separate living areas, and the Non Nuoc beachfront is quieter and cleaner than central My Khe. Marble Mountains are 5 minutes by taxi.

Best for: families who want a full waterpark experience combined with five-star service.

Premier Village Da Nang Resort

#3 Family Pick Villa Style

Premier Village Da Nang Resort

★★★★★
📍 My Khe Beach, Da Nang
Private Pool Villas Kids Club Beachfront Connecting Rooms

Premier Village offers the most private family experience in Da Nang — 110 beachfront villas, each with a private plunge pool and direct beach access. Three-bedroom villas accommodate larger families comfortably with full kitchens and separate kids' bedrooms. The resort's children's club, splash pad, and dedicated family pool make it a genuinely self-contained option. Set on the southern end of My Khe Beach with Marble Mountains visible from the shoreline.

Best for: families wanting private villa space and a plunge pool without sharing with the general hotel population.

Family Hotels Da Nang — FAQs

Which Da Nang hotel is best for families with young children?
The Hyatt Regency Da Nang is the top pick for young children, with its waterslide pool, shallow splash zone, lazy river, and Camp Hyatt programme for ages 3–12. Furama Resort is the close second with three pools and a dedicated kids' club.
Do Da Nang family hotels have kids clubs?
All four hotels in this guide operate supervised kids clubs: Furama Kids' Club, Camp Hyatt, Premier Village's children's club, and Pullman's Muse Kids Club. Most run daily programmes for ages 4–12 with activities including arts, water games, and local cultural crafts.
What is the best beach for families in Da Nang?
Non Nuoc Beach (where the Hyatt Regency and Premier Village are located) is generally calmer and less crowded than central My Khe Beach, making it the better choice for families with young children. The water is usually gentler outside of typhoon season (October–November).
When is the best time to visit Da Nang with kids?
March through August is the dry season and best window for families. June and July are peak season with calm seas and reliable sunshine. Avoid October–November when typhoon-season swells can make beach swimming unsafe.
Are Da Nang family resorts close to Ba Na Hills?
Ba Na Hills is 30–45 minutes by taxi or resort transfer from all My Khe and Non Nuoc Beach hotels. Most resorts offer organised day-trip transfers. Marble Mountains are much closer — approximately 10 minutes from the Non Nuoc hotels.
