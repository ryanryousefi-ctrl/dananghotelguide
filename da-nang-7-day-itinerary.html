Seven days in Da Nang is enough to do everything properly ‚Äî and "properly" is the operating word. Three to four days covers the headline attractions. The remaining days allow for the slower pleasures that define the destination: a full morning on the beach with nowhere to be, an early motorbike ride to Son Tra before the heat builds, a long lunch that becomes dinner in Hoi An.

This itinerary balances the three things Da Nang does best ‚Äî beach, culture, and food ‚Äî with the two essential day trips (Hoi An and Ba Na Hills) and leaves Day 7 genuinely flexible. Use it as a template and adjust based on your pace, interests, and how seriously you take afternoon naps.

Best Areas to Stay for a 7-Day Trip

For this itinerary, My Khe Beach (An Thuong area) is the optimal base for all 7 nights. When This Itinerary Works Best

March to May is the optimal window ‚Äî warm and sunny (28‚Äì33¬∞C), beach conditions consistently good, Ba Na Hills uncrowded on weekdays, and hotel prices near annual minimums. The Hoi An day trip is most enjoyable before high summer crowds peak.

June to August works well for beach-focused travellers despite higher temperatures (35‚Äì38¬∞C). The sea is flat, swimming conditions are excellent, and the beach culture is at its liveliest. Ba Na Hills and Marble Mountains require very early starts to beat the heat. Book hotels at least 4‚Äì6 weeks in advance for this period.

December to February is dry and good for sightseeing with cooler temperatures (22‚Äì28¬∞C) ‚Äî ideal for Ba Na Hills and Marble Mountains, less ideal for prolonged beach swimming. Hotel prices spike around Christmas, New Year, and T·∫øt. Book 8‚Äì12 weeks in advance for these peak dates.

Common Questions

7 Days in Da Nang: FAQ

Is 7 days too long in Da Nang?
Not at all ‚Äî 7 days is the ideal length for experiencing Da Nang and the surrounding region properly. Three to four days covers the city's main attractions. The remaining days allow for Hoi An (1‚Äì2 nights or a full day trip), a Son Tra Peninsula morning, and optionally a Hue day trip. Visitors who spend 7 days consistently report having enough time to relax between activities ‚Äî which is qualitatively different from a 3-night sprint through the highlights. If you have fewer than 5 days, the itinerary compresses significantly and becomes rushed.
Can you do Hue in one day from Da Nang?
Yes, but it requires an early start. Hue is 100km north ‚Äî approximately 2 hours by private car via the Hai Van Pass (scenic) or 1h 40min via tunnel (faster). A day trip covers the Hai Van Pass viewpoint, the Imperial Citadel, the Thien Mu Pagoda, and lunch. Leave Da Nang by 7am and return by 7‚Äì8pm. A private car costs 800,000‚Äì1,200,000 VND. The train (2h 30min, 80,000‚Äì150,000 VND) is a scenic alternative if you're comfortable navigating Hue independently.
Is Hoi An worth staying overnight?
Yes, if your schedule allows. Hoi An's best qualities ‚Äî the lantern-lit Ancient Town after dark and the streets before 8am ‚Äî are only accessible with an overnight stay. One night is the minimum to add the evening atmosphere; two nights allows An Bang Beach and a cooking class. If all 7 nights are already booked in Da Nang, Hoi An works as a day trip ‚Äî just stay for dinner and the evening lights before your Grab back.
What is the best time of year for this 7-day itinerary?
March to May is the best window: warm and sunny (28‚Äì33¬∞C), low-to-medium crowds, beach conditions consistently good, and hotel prices near annual minimums. June to August is best for beach weather but brings higher temperatures (35‚Äì38¬∞C) and peak crowds. December to February is dry and good for sightseeing with cooler temperatures. Avoid October and November ‚Äî Da Nang's rainy season affects several of the outdoor-focused days significantly. See our best time to visit guide for full detail.
Is Da Nang expensive for a week?
Da Nang is moderately affordable for a week. A mid-range 7-night trip (4-star My Khe hotel, mix of local and tourist restaurants, Grab transport, all main activities) costs approximately USD 840‚Äì1,210 per person excluding international flights. Backpackers at guesthouses eating local can cover the same itinerary for USD 500‚Äì750. Luxury travellers at 5-star resorts should budget USD 1,560‚Äì3,000. The single biggest cost variable is Ba Na Hills at 750,000‚Äì1,100,000 VND in tickets alone. See our budget guide for full breakdowns.
