Understanding Da Nang's climate

Da Nang's weather is shaped by two competing forces: the southwest monsoon that drives the long dry season from February through August, and the northeast monsoon that reverses in September and brings the city's dramatic rainy season through November. This binary pattern is more pronounced than in many Southeast Asian cities â€” the swing from a bone-dry March (22mm of rain) to a thoroughly saturated October (620mm) is stark, and the sea conditions follow in lockstep.

The result is a destination with an unusually long viable travel window â€” roughly seven months of genuinely good conditions â€” but a rainy season that is harder to dismiss than the "afternoon showers" framing common in Thailand or Bali. Da Nang in October is categorically different from Da Nang in April. Both are the same city, but the travel experience is not comparable. The data in this guide reflects long-term meteorological averages; individual years vary, and typhoon events can significantly alter the Septemberâ€“November picture in any given year.
