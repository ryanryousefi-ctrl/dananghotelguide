Money-Saving Tips

üçú
Eat where locals eat ‚Äî not where tourists sit
The best m√¨ Qu·∫£ng in Da Nang costs 50,000 VND at a plastic-stool shop with no English menu. The same dish on An Thuong costs 120,000 VND. Google Maps reviews in Vietnamese, not English, consistently point to the better and cheaper spots.

Hire a motorbike instead of using Grab daily
A motorbike costs 120,000 VND/day. Four Grab trips costs 180,000‚Äì360,000 VND. If you're comfortable riding, the motorbike pays for itself by mid-morning. Son Tra Peninsula in particular is far better explored on your own schedule than in a taxi.

Visit Ba Na Hills on a weekday in low season
Ba Na Hills ticket prices don't change by day, but the experience does dramatically. Weekend visits in high season (Dec‚ÄìMar) mean long queues at the Golden Bridge. A Tuesday in October costs the same but feels like a different park entirely. Check our Ba Na Hills crowd calendar for the best windows.
üí±
Exchange cash at city offices, not at your hotel
Hotel exchange rates are consistently 3‚Äì5% worse than Vietcombank or the independent exchange offices on the tourist strip. On a $500 exchange this is $15‚Äì25 lost for no reason. Bring USD or EUR in cash ‚Äî these get the best rates. GBP and AUD are accepted but rates are slightly worse.
üèñ
Book hotels for March‚ÄìMay or September‚ÄìOctober
Peak season (December‚ÄìFebruary and June‚ÄìAugust) pushes hotel prices up 30‚Äì60% from shoulder rates. March‚ÄìMay offers the best combination of good weather, low crowds, and hotel prices at their annual minimum. See our Hotel Price Index for month-by-month data.
üéü
The Marble Mountains, Cham Museum, and Son Tra are all nearly free
Three of Da Nang's best cultural experiences cost a combined 100,000 VND (USD 4). Visitors who don't realise this sometimes pay for organised tours that add transport markup and a guide when self-navigation is easy and far cheaper. See our Marble Mountains guide for the DIY route.

Da Nang Costs: FAQ

How much does a trip to Da Nang cost per day?
Daily costs depend on travel style. Backpackers spending carefully manage on USD 35‚Äì50/day with a dorm or budget guesthouse, street food, motorbike hire, and free beach time. Moderately ‚Äî cheaper than Bali, Bangkok, and Singapore, comparable to Chiang Mai and Hanoi, more expensive than Cambodia or Laos. Accommodation is competitive due to high hotel supply.
