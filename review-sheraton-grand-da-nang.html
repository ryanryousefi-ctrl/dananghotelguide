Sheraton Grand Danang Resort Review

From $160/night ★ 8.7 Booking.com Reviewed 2026

Overview

The Sheraton Grand Danang Resort is part of Marriott's upper-upscale Sheraton brand and sits on a long private plot along My Khe Beach. Opened in 2018, the resort is one of the newer large-scale properties on the strip and consistently scores well on Booking.com and TripAdvisor, particularly for families and couples on week-long holidays. It has 256 rooms and suites, a large beachfront pool, a full spa, and multiple dining options.

As a Marriott Bonvoy property it attracts a significant share of loyalty programme travellers — points can be earned and redeemed here, and Bonvoy Elite members receive breakfast and room upgrades subject to availability. The resort's family-friendly infrastructure makes it one of the better-positioned hotels in Da Nang for travellers with children.

Location

The resort sits on My Khe Beach, approximately 7 km from Dragon Bridge and 4 km south of the Hilton Da Nang. It is in the more developed southern section of the My Khe strip, where the hotel density is higher and a wider range of seafood restaurants line the road. The local restaurant and bar scene is walkable, giving it better dining access than more isolated resort properties.

Da Nang Airport is 9 km away — a 15-minute taxi ride. Hoi An is approximately 25 km south, making day trips straightforward. The Marble Mountains are a 15-minute taxi ride.

  • Airport: 9 km / 15 min taxi
  • Dragon Bridge: 15 min taxi
  • Marble Mountains: 15 min taxi
  • Hoi An: 35 min by car
  • Beach restaurants: 5–10 min walk

Rooms and Design

The 256 rooms and suites use a calm, contemporary resort aesthetic with white tones, rattan accents, and large ocean-facing windows. Entry-level Superior rooms are around 45 square metres with a balcony. Ocean-view rooms on higher floors are the most popular category, offering clear views across the pool and beach to the sea. Family suites are available with separate sleeping areas.

Room maintenance is consistently cited as a strong point in guest reviews. Bathrooms are well-appointed with a bathtub in higher categories. The balconies, while not enormous, are usable for morning coffee or evening drinks.

Pools, Beach, and Amenities

The main pool is a large lagoon-style design directly adjacent to the beach, with a children's pool section and dedicated sun lounger areas. A separate indoor pool is available for cooler months. The beach in front of the resort is wide and clean, with full towel and umbrella service for guests.

The Shine Spa has a full treatment menu. A well-equipped fitness centre, tennis court, beach volleyball, watersports, and a kids' club with dedicated activity programmes round out the amenities. The kids' club is particularly well-regarded by families.

Dining Options

Market 39 is the main all-day dining restaurant, serving a full international and Vietnamese breakfast buffet and à la carte lunch and dinner. Chill Skybar is a rooftop venue with cocktails and bar snacks and good city views. A beachside bar serves light meals and drinks throughout the day.

The breakfast buffet is one of the stronger hotel breakfasts in Da Nang, with a wide selection of international and Vietnamese options. Room service is available 24 hours.

Typical Price Range

  • Low season (May–August): $160–$210/night
  • Shoulder season: $210–$290/night
  • Peak season (December–February): $290–$420/night
  • Family suites: from $320/night in low season

Who This Hotel Is Best For

The Sheraton Grand Danang is particularly well-suited to families with children, Marriott Bonvoy loyalty members, and couples on extended beach holidays. The combination of a strong kids' club, large pool, and direct beach access makes it one of the more family-friendly five-star options in Da Nang. It also works well for those who want a proper resort experience without being as isolated as the InterContinental on Son Tra Peninsula.

Pros
  • Direct My Khe Beach access with well-maintained beach facilities
  • Strong family infrastructure including dedicated kids' club
  • Marriott Bonvoy points earned; breakfast for Elite members
  • Walkable to local restaurants on the My Khe strip
  • Well-maintained rooms with consistent quality reports
  • Good location for day trips to both Da Nang city and Hoi An
Cons
  • Rates higher than comparable non-branded beachfront hotels
  • Pool area busy during Vietnamese public holidays
  • On-site dining competent but not a destination in itself
  • City-centre access requires a 15-minute taxi

Final Verdict

The Sheraton Grand Danang Resort delivers exactly what a good upper-upscale beach resort should: reliable quality, strong amenities, genuine beachfront access, and the infrastructure to keep both adults and children comfortable. For Marriott Bonvoy members in particular, it is one of the most sensible redemption options in Da Nang. Even at paid rates, it justifies its position as one of the My Khe strip's better hotels.

Is the Sheraton Grand Danang Resort Worth It?

Yes — the Sheraton Grand is one of the most underrated luxury resort hotels in Da Nang. It sits at a price point below the InterContinental and Hyatt Regency, yet delivers a beach resort experience that rivals both: a spectacular pool complex, a long stretch of My Khe Beach frontage, and rooms that are consistently praised for their quality and views. The My Khe Beach location at the quieter central section of the strip avoids both the isolation of the southern resorts and the noise of the northern tourist cluster. Marriott Bonvoy members will find the points earning strong. The main gap is in dining — on-site options are solid rather than exceptional. But at 2026 rates, the Sheraton Grand represents excellent value for a genuine beachfront luxury stay.

Nearby Attractions & Getting Around

One of Da Nang's advantages as a destination is how compact it is — most major attractions are within 20–30 minutes of any hotel in the city. From Sheraton Grand Danang Resort, key landmarks and beaches are easy to reach by taxi or GrabCar, and both services are reliable, metered, and inexpensive by international standards.

  • My Khe Beach — On-site. Direct beachfront access — wide sand and clear water on Da Nang's main coast.
  • Dragon Bridge — 15 min taxi. The city's most photographed bridge — fire show Friday and Saturday nights.
  • Han River Waterfront — 15 min taxi. Evening dining and promenade along Da Nang's central riverside.
  • Da Nang Cathedral — 15 min taxi. The distinctive pink church is a Da Nang landmark in the city centre.
  • Marble Mountains — 20 min taxi south. Five marble and limestone formations with Buddhist shrines and sea caves.
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — 30 min by car. Essential UNESCO World Heritage day trip — easy to arrange from the hotel.
